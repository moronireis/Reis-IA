M and D share a center vertical stroke — the bride and groom quite literally entwined into one gesture. Hairline 1.25px strokes with round caps and fine serifs reference old European engraving plates. The tiny dot at the junction is the moment they meet. Custom path, no font dependency.

Double-rule hairline oval — the engraved seal without being a literal wax stamp. Four tiny diamond accents at cardinal points echo compass roses on Venetian stationery. The Roman numeral date 12·VI·MMXXVI anchors it as a wedding artifact. Ideal for save-the-date stamp, letter seal, closing mark.

One continuous gesture: M rises, valleys, peaks, and the peak becomes the stem of D which sweeps right into the bowl. Abstracted to the edge of legibility on purpose — designed for favicon, loader, watermark. Reads as a mark, not as letters, which is exactly what a secondary identity should do.
