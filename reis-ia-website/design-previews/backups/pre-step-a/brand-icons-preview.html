Rationale

Design Rationale

Variation A — Recommended
Recenters the intersection at exactly y=32 (true midpoint). Widens the gap from ~5.8 to 8 units for better small-size legibility. Pushes bars closer to viewBox edges (y=8/56) for maximum use of the canvas. This is the subtlest change but the most impactful at small sizes.

Reference learning: Apple's SF Symbols adjusts proportions per optical size. Porsche's crest uses precise centering within its shield. Linear's mark is exactly centered in its container.
Variation B — Variable Stroke
Creates internal hierarchy within the mark by varying stroke weight: bars thin (1.25px), full diagonal standard (1.5px), broken diagonal segments bolder (1.75px). The eye is drawn to the intersection — the conceptual heart of the mark.

Trade-off: Adds complexity. At small sizes the weight variation may not be perceptible. Best suited for 48px+ contexts.
Variation C — Connected Path
Renders the top bar, full diagonal, and bottom bar as one continuous SVG path instead of separate lines. This produces smoother joins at the corners and a more "drawn" quality. The broken diagonal segments remain separate.

Reference learning: Stripe's mark uses continuous paths with no visible joins. Linear's mark is a single closed path.
Variation D — Taller Proportions
Narrower bars (30 vs 36 units), taller active area. The hourglass reads more like a timepiece, reinforcing the TIME metaphor. The aspect ratio moves toward 1:1.73 (approaching golden ratio territory).

Trade-off: May feel too narrow at very small sizes. Works best at 48px+.
Variation E — Maximum Negative Space
The gap is widened to ~14 units so the two triangles read almost as independent shapes. The "open passage" between them becomes the dominant visual feature, making the intersection metaphor unmissable.

Trade-off: The hourglass silhouette may be less immediately recognizable since the triangles are more separated.
Size Optimization System
Six size-specific variants with optical adjustments. Stroke weight scales inversely with size: 3px at 16px, 1.25px at 128px+. Gap width also adjusts — wider at small sizes for legibility, tighter at large for elegance.

Reference learning: Apple provides Ultralight/Thin/Light/Regular/Medium/Semibold/Bold/Heavy/Black weights per icon. We provide size-optimized weight per target rendering size.
