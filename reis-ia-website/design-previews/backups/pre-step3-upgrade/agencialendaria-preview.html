Card Components

Dark cards with generous padding, near-invisible borders, and scale+shadow hover effect.

◆
AI Strategy
Cards use 45px padding and 50px internal gap. The border at white/5% is barely visible until hover raises it to white/10%. Hover scales 1.015x with 500ms cubic-bezier(0.7, 0, 0, 1).
●
Implementation
The 10px border-radius gives a tighter, more architectural feel than typical SaaS cards (which use 16-24px). Combined with the generous padding, cards feel like panels rather than containers.
△
Optimization
Background is #141414 -- two stops above the page background (#080808). This creates enough contrast to define the card boundary even without borders, working as a complement to the border rather than relying on it.

1. Ambient Light Pool Backgrounds
Each section has a different radial gradient positioned at a corner, simulating off-screen light sources illuminating a dark room. The gradient center is 10-15% opacity white, sized at 25-50% of section width. Light source position alternates between sections (BL, TR, BR, TL) to create natural visual rhythm. This eliminates the "flat black wall" problem in dark mode.
Reis IA adaptation: Directly applicable. Alternate light pool positions across sections. Add optional gold tinting: radial-gradient(35% 50% at 0% 100%, rgba(201,168,76,0.04) 0%, #000000 100%). Use neutral (white) pools for most sections, gold-tinted pools for CTA or hero sections only. Maximum one gold pool per page.

2. Multi-Layer Dark Depth (8 Tiers)
Rather than relying on shadows (which don't work well on dark backgrounds), depth perception comes from background color differentiation alone. Darker values recede, lighter values advance. This 8-tier system allows very fine control over spatial hierarchy.
Reis IA adaptation: Expand current 3-tier system (#000, #0A0A0A, #141414) to 5 tiers by adding #0D0D0D and #111111 as intermediates. 5 tiers is sufficient for Reis IA's simpler component hierarchy -- 8 is overkill for a consultancy site. Map: page=#000, section-alt=#0A0A0A, card-bg=#111111, elevated=#141414, hover=#1A1A1A.
3. Warm-Tinted Dark Gradients
Buttons and card backgrounds use dark gradients with warm brown tinting (#36332f, #12110d) rather than neutral gray/black. This adds subliminal warmth that complements the gold accent without being overtly gold. It's the visual equivalent of warm lighting in an interior space.
Reis IA adaptation: Use on ghost/secondary buttons and card hover states. Replace brown tints with ultra-subtle gold: linear-gradient(99deg, rgba(201,168,76,0.06), rgba(0,0,0,0.4)). Reserve solid gold background for primary CTAs only -- the warm gradient becomes the "middle tier" between ghost and primary.

4. Section Edge Gradient Masks
Sections fade into each other using CSS mask-image gradients rather than hard edges. The mask creates transparent zones at top and bottom of each section, making content appear to emerge from and dissolve into darkness. This is more sophisticated than simple bottom-fades because the mask affects all content, not just the background.
Reis IA adaptation: Use sparingly -- perhaps only on the hero-to-first-section transition. Over-using section fades weakens the architectural, grid-based feel that Reis IA targets. A single gradient fade at the hero bottom is elegant; applying it everywhere would feel soft and formless.
