Reis IA Adaptation

Replace #0CC481 with our muted gold #C9A84C. Replace #0F1C16 with #1A1A0A (dark gold-adjacent). Signature Elements

The three defining visual characteristics that make Morningside's design recognizable. Each includes an interactive demo and Reis IA adaptation strategy.

1. Grain Texture Overlay

Texture adds warmth
A subtle grain pattern transforms flat dark backgrounds into surfaces with depth and analog warmth. The effect is barely visible but deeply felt.
SVG feTurbulence filter | opacity: 0.03-0.05 | background-size: 256px
Reis IA Adaptation

Implement grain via inline SVG data URI (no external file dependency). Apply to all section containers at 3.5% opacity. This is the highest-impact visual upgrade for our dark backgrounds -- prevents the "flat void" problem and adds premium texture. Use the CSS snippet from snippets-morningside.md #4.

Reis IA Adaptation

Direct color swap: green -> gold. The technique is identical. Gradient runs from #C9A84C (bright gold) to #1A1A0A (near-black with warm tint). Combined with the gold glow shadow, this creates our signature card hover treatment. Apply to pillar cards, case study cards, and pricing tiers.

3. Single-Accent Discipline

Morningside: 1 Accent
Only green stands out
Reis IA: 1 Accent
Only gold stands out
Both sites use ONE accent color on a black/white neutral base. Every instance of the accent immediately reads as "important." This extreme restraint creates stronger brand recognition than a multi-color palette.
Reis IA Adaptation

This validates our existing approach. The competitive advantage Reis IA has over Morningside is proprietary iconography (hourglass + chess) -- they rely solely on color for brand differentiation, while we have color + motifs. Never introduce a secondary accent color. If a visual needs differentiation, use opacity/size variations of gold, not a new color.
