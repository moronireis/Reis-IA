## 1. Core Model

The paid launch inverts traditional lead generation. Instead of paying for leads and then selling, the lead PAYS to access your event. This ticket revenue funds traffic, making scale theoretically infinite.

### Business Equation

```
Ticket Revenue = Traffic Budget (ROAS >= 1.0)
Main Product Revenue = Pure Profit (~35% net margin after taxes, team, tools)
```

### Why It Works

1. **Qualified leads** — people who paid are 3-10x more likely to buy the main product
2. **Commitment** — paying eliminates no-shows and casual signups
3. **Peace of mind** — traffic is pre-funded, no risk of negative ROAS on launch
4. **Repeatability** — same recorded event runs weekly/monthly with optimization
5. **Profitable from day one** — ticket sales cover costs immediately
6. **Low risk** — unlike traditional launches where you invest R$500K+ hoping for return

### Revenue Results (Benchmark)

- Weekly launches: R$280K/week at scale
- Monthly: R$1M+/month from paid launches alone
- Growth rate: 10-15% month-over-month with same recorded event
- Net margin: ~35% after all costs
- ROAS target: 2.0x (where ticket revenue funds traffic, so effective ROAS on personal capital = infinite)

## 2. Revenue Stack Architecture

### Ticket Pricing

| Niche Type | Recommended Ticket | Rationale |
|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Large niche (fitness, money) | R$17-R$27 | Volume play — more leads, lower barrier |
| Medium niche | R$35-R$47 | Balance of volume and qualification |
| Specific niche | R$47-R$62 | Higher qualification, fewer but better leads |

### Order Bump Pyramid (Descending)

```
Base Ticket:  R$62
Bump 1:       R$35 (complementary resource)
Bump 2:       R$17 (quick-win tool)
─────────────────────
Average Ticket: R$90-95
```

- Max 3 order bumps (tested 5 — results worse)
- Always descending price (most expensive first)
- Each bump must add clear, independent value

### Optional Upsell

- R$98 VIP access (group sessions, books, priority support)
- Presented on thank-you page or onboarding
- Pure profit — not necessary for model to work

### Main Product

- Sold during/after event (lessons 4-7 of challenge, or during workshop)
- Price: R$297-R$2,000+ depending on niche
- Conversion target: 7%+ of ticket buyers

## 3. Funnel Architecture

```
[Creative Ad] → [Landing Page] → [Checkout + Order Bumps] → [Thank You]
                                                                  ↓
                                                        [WhatsApp Onboarding]
                                                                  ↓
                                                        [Warm-up Sequence]
                                                                  ↓
                                                        [Event (Workshop or Challenge)]
                                                                  ↓
                                                        [Pitch → Main Product Sale]
                                                                  ↓
                                                        [Cart Open (Monday after event)]
```

### Landing Page Conversion Benchmarks

| Metric | Excellent | OK | Needs Work |
|--------|-----------|-----|------------|
| Visits → Purchase | >10% | 7-9% | <5% |

**Important**: Measure visits-to-PURCHASE, not page-to-checkout. Page-to-checkout can be misleading (35% clickthrough but only 5% checkout completion = page has clickbait problem).

### Checkout Conversion

- Validated checkouts convert 7-10%
- Personalized checkout (with promise reminder, bullets, guarantee) outperforms default by 30-40%
- Test at least 2 checkouts per week

### Thank You Page → WhatsApp Onboarding

- Don't rely on thank-you page for surveys (Pix buyers often don't see it)
- Move all onboarding to WhatsApp: group entry, survey, lesson schedule, engagement setup

## 4. Traffic Strategy: 6 Scaling Phases

### Phase 1 — Testing (ASC Campaign)

**Structure**: 1 campaign → 1 ad set (open audience with light direction) → 15 creatives (10 static + 5 video)

**Simultaneous testing**:
- Test creatives + pages together (not isolated like traditional launches)
- Rotate 4 pages through same creatives to find creative-page affinity
- Then rotate checkouts through validated creative-page combos

**Budget**: CPM × 15 (e.g., CPM R$20 → R$300/day minimum)
- Low budget alternative: R$50/day (slower learning, but works)

**Pause rule**: If CAC reaches 3× ticket price with zero sales → pause immediately

**Validation**: 5,000+ impressions + CAC < ticket price

**Key insight**: Creative 1,3,5 may convert with Page 1 but not Page 2. Creative 2,4,6 may convert with Page 2. Don't discard creatives — test with different pages first.

### Phase 2 — Initial Scale (ASC)

- 5 validated creatives only per campaign
- R$500/day budget
- Keep Phase 1 testing campaigns running in parallel
- Multiple campaigns = parallel validation

### Phase 3 — Audience Testing (CBO) — >R$10K/day threshold

CBO campaigns with warm audiences:
- Ad Set 1: Instagram engagement 7 days
- Ad Set 2: Saved posts 30 days
- Ad Set 3: Challenge/launch leads
- Ad Set 4: Instagram followers

Also test cold audiences:
- Lookalike 1% of buyers
- Lookalike 1% of product buyers (need 2K+ source)
- Interest-based targeting

### Phase 4 — CBO Scaling

- 30-50% more budget than Phase 3
- Only validated creatives + audiences from Phase 3
- Isolate audiences per ad set

### Phase 5 — Multi-Campaign Horizontal Scale

- 30+ ASC campaign variations
- Each = different creative + page + checkout combo
- Budget per campaign: R$500-R$2,500/day
- **Automated rules**:
  - Good CAC → increase budget 15-20% per hour
  - Bad CAC → decrease budget 15% per hour
  - Creative with R$100+ spend and zero sales → auto-pause
- Can use multiple Business Managers (BMs) simultaneously

### Phase 6 — Master Phase

- ASC with 30 validated creatives (minimum)
- Budget R$5K-R$20K/day per campaign
- At 300 sales/day per account → duplicate best campaigns to new ad account
- Ad accounts have a ceiling; replicating across accounts breaks through it
- "Let it burn" mode — fully validated, automated optimization

## 5. Testing Cadence

| Asset | Minimum Tests/Week | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| Pages | 4 | Never stop — page fatigue is real |
| Checkouts | 2 | Small changes = big conversion shifts |
| Creatives | Continuous | Creative is king for scale |
| Prices | Monthly | Test R$17, R$27, R$47, R$62 |

**Rule**: If you stop testing pages, you lose scale. Change pages before performance drops, not after.

## 6. Key Metrics Dashboard

### Traffic Metrics
- CPM, CTR, CPC
- CAC (cost per ticket acquisition)
- ROAS (minimum 1.0, target 2.0)

### Funnel Metrics
- Page visits → purchases (conversion rate)
- Checkout initiated → completed
- Average ticket (base + order bumps)
- Refund/cancellation rate

### Event Metrics
- Daily unique view rate (target: >70%)
- Live attendance: Day 1 ~30%, Day 7 ~15% (normal)
- Secret room participation (target: >40%)
- Drop-off identification by lesson
- Chat engagement and testimonial volume

### Conversion Metrics
- Conversion to main product: target 7%+
- Revenue per ticket buyer
- Total ROAS (ticket + product revenue / total ad spend)

## 7. Optimization Protocol

When metrics are available, analyze in this order:

1. **Executive summary** — what's happening overall
2. **Funnel reading** — where are people dropping
3. **Primary bottleneck** — the #1 thing limiting growth
4. **Secondary bottleneck** — the #2 thing
5. **Probable causes** — classified by confidence (high/medium/low)
6. **What's working** — don't break what works
7. **What's wasting** — budget or energy being burned
8. **Keep / Pause / Test** — clear action categories
9. **Prioritized action plan** — ordered by impact × ease
10. **Quick win** — the single change with highest probable impact

## 8. Common Pitfalls

1. **Analyzing ROAS in isolation** — a low-ROAS creative may be nurturing top-of-funnel that converts via other ads later (Andromeda sequencing)
2. **Killing creatives too early** — need 5K+ impressions before judgment
3. **Not testing pages** — same creative converts differently on different pages
4. **Copying examples instead of modeling** — direct copies always underperform
5. **Single winning ad mentality** — post-Andromeda requires a portfolio (10-50 ads)
6. **Spending own money on traffic** — breaks the fundamental premise
7. **Overcomplicating the event** — simple, direct, results-focused events convert best
8. **Not delivering real transformation** — if attendees don't get results during event, they won't buy
