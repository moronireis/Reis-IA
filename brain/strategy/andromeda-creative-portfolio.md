## 1. What Changed with Andromeda

Meta partnered with Nvidia for massive hardware + AI upgrades. The algorithm now understands individual user journeys in real-time.

### Before Andromeda (Old Model)
- Search for 1 "winning ad"
- Duplicate winner into clusters
- Scale single best-performing creative
- Isolated testing (1 ad, 1 audience, measure separately)
- Algorithm grouped people by segments, tested CPM-based

### After Andromeda (New Model)
- Algorithm sequences DIFFERENT ads to the SAME person based on their behavior
- Tests different orders: Ad-A → Ad-B → Ad-C vs Ad-B → Ad-C → Ad-A (varies per user)
- Portfolio of strategic ads > single winner
- Clusters (duplicates of 1 ad) = treated as spam
- Top-of-funnel ads may have LOW ROAS but FEED the conversion of other ads
- **Don't pause "low ROAS" ads if they're warming cold audiences**

### Practical Shift
- **Old**: Find 1 Michael Jordan ad, scale it
- **New**: Have 10-50 different strategic ads covering different angles, hooks, and awareness stages

## 2. The 10 Strategic Ad Types

Every portfolio should cover these 10 types. The algorithm will sequence them per user for maximum conversion.

| # | Type | Purpose | Funnel Role |
|---|------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Hook-based** | Pattern-break, stop thumbs | Top of funnel |
| 2 | **Pain/Problem** | Touch nerve, create identification | Awareness |
| 3 | **Insight/Educational** | Teach something valuable | Consideration |
| 4 | **Direct offer** | Sales-driven with price and CTA | Bottom of funnel |
| 5 | **Smart choice** | Comparison vs alternatives | Consideration |
| 6 | **Inside method** | Show course/platform preview | Consideration |
| 7 | **Real results** | Testimonials, transformations, before/after | Trust-building |
| 8 | **Mental unlock** | Remove limiting belief | Objection-breaking |
| 9 | **Now-or-never** | Urgency, scarcity, last call | Conversion push |
| 10 | **Behind-the-scenes** | Authenticity, raw, unpolished | Trust-building |

### Additional Variations
- **Invisible error** — "The mistake you don't know you're making"
- **Counterintuitive** — "Stop doing X if you want Y"
- **Specific objection** — directly addresses one common objection
- **Event invitation** — "60,000 people already paid for this"

## 3. Format Mix

### Optimal Distribution
- **60% static/image** — works as remarketing, fast to produce, easy to test
- **40% video** — works as conversion, deeper engagement, but harder to produce

### Static Creative Structure
```
[Promise headline]
[Expert photo OR testimonial photo]
[Social proof element]
[CTA button/text]
```

Best for: remarketing (person already saw videos, static reinforces message repeatedly)

### Video Creative Structure
```
[Hook — 3-5 seconds] ← 80% of the work
[Development — argue, show pains, build desire]
[Proof — testimonial, result, credibility]
[CTA — clear, simple next step]
```

**Rule**: First 3-5 seconds = everything. If the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.

### Video Format Types
- Transitions (dynamic cuts, visual hooks)
- Q&A boxes (native Instagram format, passes as organic)
- Pain/Problem focused (strong visual + emotional hook)
- Before/After (transformation proof)
- Podcast-style (two people, conversational)
- Expert entering car/daily life (raw, authentic — often outperforms polished)

## 4. Hook Architecture

### The 80/20 Rule
The hook IS the creative. Create 3 hooks for 1 body to test communication lines faster. This saves production time and validates faster.

### Hook Angle Types

| Angle | Example | Mechanism |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Counterintuitive** | "Stop eating meat if you want real weight loss" | Inverts belief |
| **Paradoxical** | "Why does your friend eat lots but stay thin?" | Creates curiosity gap |
| **Curiosity direct** | "The invisible error killing your launch" | Must-know feeling |
| **Proof/Social** | "60K people already paid for this challenge" | Social validation |
| **Urgency** | "Last call — enrollments close today" | Fear of missing out |
| **Pain-driven** | "Tired of explaining to the board why AI didn't deliver?" | Identification |
| **Results-proof** | "She lost 8kg in 7 days. Here's the exact method" | Aspirational proof |
| **Alert** | "Warning: this affects 87% of entrepreneurs" | Pattern interrupt |

## 5. Campaign Structure (Andromeda-Optimized)

### ASC (Advantage Shopping Campaign) — Primary

```
Campaign (ASC / Advantage+)
└── 1 Ad Set (open audience, light direction)
    ├── Creative 1 (static — hook type)
    ├── Creative 2 (video — pain type)
    ├── Creative 3 (static — testimonial)
    ├── Creative 4 (video — educational)
    ├── Creative 5 (static — direct offer)
    ├── ... up to 15 creatives per campaign
    └── Creative 15 (video — urgency)
```

### Key Rules
- 1 ad set per campaign (open to all, light demographic direction)
- 15 creatives per campaign (10 static + 5 video)
- Let the algorithm optimize — don't micro-manage audience
- Separate campaigns for: new customers vs existing customers
- Never dump untested creatives into winning campaigns — test separately, move winners

### CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) — Secondary
- Used from Phase 3+ (>R$10K/day)
- Segment by audience: warm (engagement, followers) and cold (lookalikes, interests)
- 1 audience per ad set for isolation
- Validated creatives only

## 6. Creative Validation Protocol

### Minimum Threshold
- **5,000+ impressions** required before any judgment
- Below 5K = not enough data, don't pause

### CAC-Based Decision
| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| CAC < ticket price | Keep running — validated |
| CAC < average ticket (with bumps) | Optimal — scale |
| CAC = 1.5x ticket, some sales | Monitor — may be warming funnel |
| CAC = 3x ticket, zero sales | Pause immediately |
| R$100+ spend, zero sales | Pause (automated rule) |

### Portfolio Analysis (Don't Judge Ads in Isolation)
- A creative with high spend but low ROAS may be nurturing top-of-funnel
- If other creatives convert AFTER the "low ROAS" one was seen → it's doing its job
- Only pause if: zero contribution to any downstream conversion

### Creative with No Spend
- Algorithm rejected it (low relevance or poor CTR predicted)
- Replace with new creative — don't try to "fix" rejected ones

## 7. Production Best Practices

1. **Volume over perfection** — polished professional videos often flop; raw authentic hooks win
2. **Never stop producing** — creative fatigue is real; always have new creatives in pipeline
3. **Model, don't copy** — direct copies of examples always underperform
4. **Use AI for ideation** — GPT helps brainstorm hooks, angles, and copy variations
5. **Test everything** — there is no formula; each niche and audience is different
6. **3 hooks per body** — fastest way to test communication lines
7. **Mix formats** — static for remarketing, video for conversion, native formats for organic feel

## 8. Scaling Creative Production

| Scale Level | Creatives Needed | Production Cadence |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Starting | 30+ initial batch | 10-15 new/week |
| Growing (R$5K/day) | 50+ active | 15-20 new/week |
| Scaling (R$10K+/day) | 100+ tested | 20-30 new/week |
| Master (R$50K+/day) | 300+ tested, 30+ validated | Continuous production team |

### Production Team Roles
- Copywriter: hooks, angles, scripts
- Video editor: cuts, transitions, captions
- Designer: static creatives, thumbnails
- Expert: raw recordings (doesn't need to be polished)
- Analyst: validation, performance tracking, winner identification
