## STRATEGIC INTENT

The Castelo dos Lagos Instagram profile is not a photo album. It is a curated walk-through of the estate — the same editorial experience the website delivers, compressed into 15-second story frames that a bride, a mother, or an event planner encounters before they save the post, before they send the link to someone, and before they request a visit.

Every highlight exists to accomplish one of three jobs:

1. **Establish the estate** — this is a castle, not a venue. Stone, wood, lakes, 16,000 m2. The physical reality does the selling.
2. **Match the visitor to a celebration type** — weddings, debutantes, anniversaries, corporate. Each one lives differently inside the castle.
3. **Route toward the visit** — the only conversion action is scheduling an in-person visit. No pricing, no packages, no booking form. Just: come see it.

**Brand rules that apply here:**
- Voice: stewardship, first-person plural ("nós" / "a casa"), PT-BR executive
- Positioning: "Estate, not venue" — never call it "espaco de eventos" or "salao de festas"
- Tone: quiet confidence. The castle speaks for itself. Copy frames, never sells.
- Anti-patterns: wedding cliches ("sonho realizado", "dia mais feliz"), emojis, gold foil aesthetic, medieval fantasy, exclamation marks, hashtag stuffing
- CTA: always "Agendar visita" — never pricing, never "solicite orcamento"
- Typography cues for designed stories: Cormorant Garamond (emotional/display) + Inter (structural/functional), forest green + gold accent palette

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Visual Standards
- All stories use 1080x1920 (9:16) format
- Typography: Cormorant Garamond italic for emotional/display text, Inter for structural/functional text
- Color palette: forest-deep (#1E2E24) backgrounds, cream (#F4EFE6) text, gold (#C9A96E) accents on labels and hairlines
- No filters that alter the natural green/stone/wood color temperature of the castle
- No text over busy areas of photos — use solid color blocks or gradient overlays with min 70% opacity
- No stickers, GIFs, polls, or Instagram native text styles — all typography must be designed externally (Canva, Figma, or code-generated)
