#### "Revenue-First" Deep Analysis

This is the flagship term. Three PT-BR adaptation options:

1. **"Receita Primeiro"** -- Direct, punchy. Mirrors the English cadence. Slight risk of sounding like a literal translation.
2. **"Foco em Receita"** -- More natural in PT-BR. Loses the "first" positioning emphasis.
3. **"Receita Antes de Tudo"** -- Most natural and confident for a Brazilian CEO audience. Conveys priority clearly.

**Recommendation**: Keep "Revenue-First" as the branded framework name (it functions as a proper noun, like "Lean Startup"). But in descriptive/contextual usage, use "Receita Antes de Tudo" or "orientado a receita". The framework itself should stay in English as a brand identifier -- it is distinctive and memorable for the BR tech/business audience.

## B) Text Density Issues

### Issue B1
**Page**: Home
**Section**: Manifesto (Section 2, lines 100-116)
**Current text**: The entire manifesto block is 15 paragraphs of continuous prose -- particularly the chess analogy paragraph: "Essa e a abordagem Revenue-First. Mapeamos o tabuleiro -- seu negocio, suas lacunas de receita, suas oportunidades de maior alavancagem -- antes de construir um unico sistema. Depois construimos com precisao cirurgica."
**Problem**: Too many ideas in one paragraph. Three concepts (mapping the board, revenue gaps, surgical precision) compressed into one sentence.
**Direction**: Split into shorter punches. Separate the analogy from the application.

### Issue B2
**Page**: Home
**Section**: Founder Story (Section 6, lines 416-425)
**Current text**: The hourglass paragraph: "Tem um recurso que nenhum sistema de AI fabrica e nenhuma quantia de dinheiro recupera: tempo. Cada semana com a abordagem errada de AI e uma semana de perda acumulada -- orcamento desperdicado, receita que nao entrou, uma distancia competitiva que cresce em silencio enquanto o prototipo acumula poeira. A Reis IA existe para fechar essa distancia. Rapido. Com proposito."
**Problem**: 4+ lines of continuous text with too many sub-ideas packed in (time, budget waste, missed revenue, competitive gap, dusty prototypes, speed, purpose).
**Direction**: Break into 2-3 shorter paragraphs. Let each idea breathe.
