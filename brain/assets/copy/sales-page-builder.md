## SECTION 5 — OBJECTION HANDLING

**Section header (internal):** [OBJECTIONS]

### "Can I really sell AI if I'm not technical?"

This is the most common question — and the most important misconception to clear up.

The best AI sellers are not the most technical people. They are the best business strategists.

Your job as the agency owner is to identify the revenue opportunity in a client's business and sell the outcome. The technical implementation is the execution layer — and that's exactly what the Builder program teaches you to structure, partner, or deliver through the Reis IA ecosystem.

83% of tech leaders outsource at least part of their AI initiatives. Even the technical people outsource. You don't need to understand neural networks. You need to understand where AI makes money — and that's a business strategy skill, not a coding skill.

---

### "What if I don't get clients during the program?"

The program is structured specifically to prevent this.

Module 2 is dedicated entirely to client acquisition. You'll build your outreach system, run live sales conversations, and get direct coaching on what to adjust. By Week 4, you should have active prospects in your pipeline.

And if you complete every module and still don't have a sellable AI offer? Full refund.

We stand behind this because the methodology works — and because we only accept people we believe will put in the work.

---

### "Is $4,000–$15,000 too much to invest right now?"

Let's do the math.

One AI project sells for $5,000–$20,000.

The Builder program pays for itself on your first client — often before the program ends.

The real question is: what does 12 more months of waiting cost you?

Every month agencies are adding AI services, raising their prices, and taking clients who used to consider you. That cost is invisible but real.

---

### "What if I don't have time while running my agency?"

Builder is designed for founders with existing businesses — not students with unlimited time.

Sessions are structured, not open-ended. Templates and frameworks cut the time it would take to figure this out alone by 80%. And the program is designed to generate revenue during the 12 weeks — so it pays for the time it requires.

If you can commit 5–8 hours per week for 12 weeks, you can do this.

---

### "I've invested in programs before and didn't get results."

That's a real concern — and I take it seriously.

Here's what's different: Builder is not a course with pre-recorded videos. It's a live mentorship with direct access, weekly accountability, and implementation support on real client projects.

The guarantee exists because I know the difference between someone who shows up and executes versus someone who buys and disappears. I only want the former — and I protect both of us with the guarantee.
