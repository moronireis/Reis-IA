**Send timing:** Day 2

Subject: 42% of AI projects fail. Here's the real reason.

---

[FIRST NAME],

42% of companies abandoned their AI projects in 2025.

Think about that number.

Almost half. Gone. Millions of dollars in technology investment sitting in a folder somewhere, labeled "AI initiative — paused."

The standard explanation is: AI is hard. The technology is immature. The implementation was complex. The vendor didn't deliver.

Those are symptoms. Here's the actual cause.

**Most AI projects start with the wrong question.**

"What AI should we build?" leads you to build something.

It doesn't lead you to build the *right* thing.

Companies spend months scoping a chatbot, building a chatbot, launching a chatbot — and six months later, nobody can tell you what the chatbot actually did for revenue. Because nobody asked that before they started.

This is what I call the AI Prototype Graveyard.

You've probably seen it. Maybe you've been in it.

The impressive demo. The vendor who spoke in technical terms you couldn't evaluate. The project that took longer than expected, cost more than quoted, and generated an implementation report nobody reads.

The Prototype Graveyard has three entry points:

**1. "AI for the sake of AI"** — implementing technology because it sounds innovative, not because there's a revenue thesis behind it.

**2. "The tech-first trap"** — starting with the tool (a specific AI platform, a specific agent type) instead of the business problem it's supposed to solve.

**3. "The generalist curse"** — working with vendors who claim to do everything for everyone, which means they've optimized for nothing specific in your business.

---

The companies that get AI right are the ones who start differently.

They don't ask "What should we build?" They ask: "Where is the revenue?"

Then they work backward from that answer to the implementation.

That's the foundation of the Revenue-First AI Framework. And it's what separates AI systems that generate measurable returns from AI systems that look impressive in a slide deck and die in production.

Tomorrow I'll show you how the framework actually works — and what the first question looks like when you apply it to a real business.

## EMAIL 3 — THE REVENUE-FIRST FRAMEWORK

**Send timing:** Day 5
**Subject line:** The only AI question that matters
**Preview text:** Start here and the rest gets easier.

---

Subject: The only AI question that matters

---

[FIRST NAME],

Here's the question.

**"Where does AI make you money?"**

Not: what can AI do for your industry.
Not: what are the most popular AI use cases.
Not: what's the most technically interesting implementation.

Where — specifically, in your specific business model — does AI create the most leverage on revenue?

That question changes everything that follows.

---

The Revenue-First AI Framework has three layers.

**Layer 1: Strategy**
Before touching any technology, map the revenue opportunity. Where in your business is AI the highest-leverage move? For most companies, this is one of three places: top of funnel (more leads, better qualified), middle of funnel (faster conversion, better follow-up), or operational (lower cost to deliver, faster response).

The AI Revenue Audit is where this happens. 60–90 minutes. A written report that maps your business against these three layers. You walk out knowing exactly what to build first and why.

**Layer 2: Implementation**
Build the AI system that connects directly to the revenue process you identified. Not a general-purpose tool. A specific system with a defined input, defined output, and defined KPI.

An AI sales agent that qualifies leads is a revenue system. A chatbot that answers FAQs might be a cost-reduction system. Both have value — but Layer 1 tells you which one to build first in your specific situation.

**Layer 3: Scale**
Create the infrastructure to extend what's working. For agencies, this is the ability to sell AI services to clients without doing every implementation yourself. For companies, this is the ability to expand AI across more processes without starting from scratch each time.

---

Most people skip Layer 1 and start at Layer 2.

That's the mistake. That's the Prototype Graveyard.

Layer 1 takes an hour. Sometimes less. The AI Revenue Audit is how we do it — and it's free for qualified companies because the output is valuable regardless of what comes next.

---

Over the next few days, I'm going to show you what Layer 2 looks like in practice — a real AI system built with this framework, with before/after results.

For now: if you want to run Layer 1 on your own business, reply to this email with "AUDIT" and I'll send you three questions to work through. Takes 20 minutes. Tells you a lot.
