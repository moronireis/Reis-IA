## a) ICP Definition — Systems Buyer

### Decision Maker Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Title | CEO, Founder, COO, VP Operations |
| Company revenue | R$5M-R$100M (sweet spot: R$10M-R$50M) |
| Team size | 10-200 employees |
| Industry | SaaS, professional services, e-commerce, education, real estate, health tech |
| Geography | Brazil (primary) |
| Technical literacy | Low to moderate — understands business outcomes, does NOT want to touch technology |
| Budget authority | R$10K-R$100K for projects that prove ROI |

### Core Identity Statement

This is NOT a person who wants to learn AI. This is a person who wants to **buy a result**. They hire accountants, lawyers, agencies — and they want to hire someone to handle AI the same way. Zero interest in understanding the technology. Full interest in understanding the revenue impact.

**Critical difference from Builder ICP**: The Builder buyer wants AUTONOMY ("teach me to fish"). The Systems buyer wants DELEGATION ("bring me the fish, cooked, on a plate, with the receipt showing how much it saved me").

### Awareness Level

**Problem-aware to Solution-aware.** They know their operations have bottlenecks. Many have heard that AI can help. Most have NOT committed to a specific approach. Some have been burned by failed implementations. All are skeptical of promises.

The page must meet them at problem-aware (lead with the pain they recognize) and move them to solution-aware (introduce the Revenue-First Framework as the mechanism) and then to product-aware (Reis IA Systems as the specific answer).

### Primary Pain — The Bleeding Neck

"Minha empresa cresce, mas as operacoes nao acompanham. Meu time de vendas gasta 70% do tempo com leads que nao vao comprar. Meu suporte responde as mesmas perguntas 200 vezes por dia. E eu sei que AI pode resolver isso — mas o ultimo projeto de AI que tentamos morreu no meio do caminho e ninguem conseguiu me mostrar um real de resultado."

In English: Operations bottlenecked by manual processes. Revenue leaking through unqualified leads, overwhelmed support, and human-dependent workflows. The pain is compounded by past failure — they tried AI (or tried automations) and it did not deliver.

### What They Have Already Tried

| Attempt | Why It Failed |
|---------|---------------|
| Zapier / Make.com | Hit the complexity ceiling. Works for simple flows, breaks for real business logic |
| Freelancer / offshore dev | Built what was asked, not what was needed. No strategic layer. Broke after handoff |
| AI agency (generic) | Started with tech, not business outcome. Impressive demo, useless in production |
| Internal team | No AI expertise. Spent months experimenting with ChatGPT prompts. No production system |
| Did nothing | Analysis paralysis. Every vendor pitch led with tech features they could not evaluate |

### Why Those Failed (The Strategic Insight)

Every failed attempt started with the TECHNOLOGY. "Let's build a chatbot." "Let's automate this workflow." "Let's implement AI." None of them started with: "Where is the revenue opportunity, and how do we measure it?"

This is the core positioning lever for the entire page. The Revenue-First Framework is not a marketing gimmick — it is the methodological answer to why everything else failed.

### Buying Triggers (What Makes Them Act NOW)

1. Missed revenue target and the board is asking questions
2. Key employee quit, exposing how fragile processes are
3. Competitor deployed AI and is visibly gaining ground
4. Support costs growing faster than revenue
5. Investor or board member directly asking about AI strategy
6. Saw a specific use case (article, event, peer conversation) and thought "we need this"

### Objections Specific to Systems Buyer

| Objection | Root Cause | How Copy Must Address |
|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| "We tried AI and it failed" | Past burn from tech-first vendor | Validate their experience (42% stat), explain WHY it failed, show how Revenue-First is different |
| "How do I know this will integrate with our systems?" | Fear of disruption | AI Revenue Audit maps tech stack before any code is written |
| "What is the ROI timeline?" | Need to justify spend to board/partners | Specific timelines: 30 days (Starter), 60 days (Full). Revenue-First Audit calculates expected ROI before build |
| "What if something breaks after handoff?" | Abandonment anxiety | 30-day support (Starter), 60-day priority support + performance guarantee (Full) |
| "R$25K-R$100K is a lot" | Comparing to DIY tools or cheaper freelancers | Reframe: cost of NOT implementing (lost leads, manual hours, competitor distance). ROI math specific to their numbers |
| "Is AI mature enough for production?" | Media hype vs reality confusion | Klarna 2.3M conversations stat. 67% specialist success rate. Production-ready for defined tasks |

### What NOT to Say (Mandatory Avoidances)

1. **Never use "learn", "aprenda", "domine", "capacite-se"** — Systems buyers do NOT want to learn anything. They want the result delivered.
2. **Never use "empreendedor" or "profissional de AI"** — wrong audience entirely. Use "executivo", "CEO", "gestor", "decisor".
3. **Never mention "autonomia" or "independencia"** — Builder language. Systems sells delegation, not autonomy.
4. **Never hype AI generically** — "revolucionario", "disruptivo", "o futuro e agora" are banned. Every AI claim must connect to a specific business outcome.
5. **Never use SaaS pricing patterns** — no pricing cards, tier comparisons, or feature checklists. Investment ranges mentioned in text only. ALL CTAs go to /agendar.
6. **Never make the reader feel they need to understand AI** — the entire page must communicate: "You do not need to understand this. We understand it. You need to understand the results."
7. **Never use "curso", "programa", "mentoria", "comunidade"** — those are Builder/Network words. Systems is "implementacao", "sistema", "projeto", "entrega".
8. **No emojis in copy.**
9. **No Azure Whisper / Blue Shimmer Text effects.**

### Entry Point: AI Revenue Audit

The AI Revenue Audit is the ONLY conversion mechanism. Every CTA on the page leads to /agendar.

**How to frame the Audit for the Systems buyer:**

The Audit is NOT a sales call. It is a diagnostic. The buyer books 60 minutes. They receive a written report showing where AI generates the most revenue in their specific business — with priority ranking, budget estimates, and timeline.

The report has standalone value. Even if they never hire Reis IA, they leave with a clear map.

This framing is critical because:
- It lowers friction (diagnostic, not sales pitch)
- It demonstrates expertise before asking for money
- It naturally bridges to the paid implementation ("now that you see the opportunity, want us to build it?")
- It disarms the skeptic ("if the audit is this good, the implementation must be real")

**Audit CTA language direction:**
- NOT: "Fale com um consultor" (generic, sounds like every agency)
- NOT: "Solicite um orcamento" (premature, they are not ready to buy)
- YES: "Agendar AI Revenue Audit" / "Descubra onde AI gera receita no seu negocio" / "Mapeie sua oportunidade de receita com AI"
- The CTA must feel like value, not commitment

### Brand Elements

- **Hourglass motif**: TIME is the premium currency for this ICP. They have money, they lack time. Every manual process is a time tax. The hourglass reinforces: time is draining, AI recovers it.
- **Chess motif**: STRATEGY before technology. The Systems buyer appreciates strategic thinking. The chess piece reinforces: this is not random implementation, this is calculated, every move intentional.
- Both must appear at least once on the page.

### Proof Strategy

Since case studies are still in placeholder stage, proof must come from:
1. **Market data** (42%, 29%, 91%, 67% vs 22%, 74% — all documented in our research)
2. **Methodology as proof** (named framework = credibility signal)

4. **Specificity as proof** (detailed process, clear deliverables, precise timelines = competence signal)

## Strategic Notes for Copywriter

1. **The page sells the AUDIT, not the implementation.** The audit is the entry point. The implementation is sold on the sales call after the audit. Do not try to close the full project on the landing page.

2. **The reader is skeptical.** They have been pitched by vendors before. The copy must feel like a peer conversation, not a sales pitch. Every claim needs a number, a specific scenario, or a named mechanism behind it.

3. **The enemy is NOT the reader's fault.** The Prototype Graveyard happened because of bad methodology from vendors — not because the reader made a bad decision. The copy must validate their experience and redirect blame to the approach, not the person.

4. **Delegation, not education.** Every sentence should reinforce: "We do this. You get the result." If any sentence makes the reader feel they need to learn or understand something technical, rewrite it.

5. **The hourglass and chess motifs are not decorative.** They carry meaning. Hourglass = time is your most scarce resource, and every manual process is stealing it. Chess = every move we make is strategic and intentional. Weave these into the copy naturally, not as forced metaphors.

6. **Consultive premium Portuguese.** Not academic. Not casual. The register is two executives having a direct, confident conversation about business results. Reference the brand voice guide in `.claude/rules/brand-voice.md`.
