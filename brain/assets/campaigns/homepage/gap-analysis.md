## SECTION-BY-SECTION ANALYSIS

---

### 1. HERO

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Dream Outcome**: The headline nails the thesis, but the subheadline (41 words) dilutes it. The CMO flagged this explicitly: "too long and tries to explain the entire ecosystem." The subheadline tries to list all three pillars + close with the revenue question — two jobs in one sentence. Result: the reader's brain works too hard after a clean headline.
- **Perceived Likelihood**: No proof element in the hero. The microcopy ("30 minutos. Sem compromisso.") handles friction but doesn't build confidence. A single credibility anchor (e.g., referencing the 91% stat or number of implementations) would increase perceived likelihood without hard-selling.
- **Time Delay**: "30 minutos" in microcopy is good but buried. The speed of getting clarity should be more prominent.
- **Effort/Sacrifice**: "Sem compromisso" is generic — every consultant says this. Needs a more specific low-effort framing.

**Energy/Flow:**
- Clean headline → dense subheadline creates a momentum drop. Reader goes from "wow" to "wait, let me re-read."
- Body copy is solid but could be tighter — "A diferença não está na tecnologia" repeats the headline's thesis instead of advancing it.

**PT-BR Issues:**
- "implementa AI em empresas, forma agências pra vender soluções de AI e constrói as parcerias que escalam tudo isso" — this reads like a translated mission statement. Too many clauses chained with "e." Brazilian executive speech uses shorter declarations.

**Revenue-First Gaps:**
- The revenue question ("onde está a receita?") is buried at the end of the subheadline instead of being the first thing the reader encounters after the headline.

**Specificity:**
- No numbers in the hero. Even one data point would ground the thesis.

---

### 2. MANIFESTO

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Status Angle (Critical)**: The reviewer scored Hormozi 8.5 specifically because status is underdeveloped. The manifesto talks about what Reis IA does and believes — but never paints the picture of who the reader BECOMES after adopting this thinking. The chess metaphor touches it but stays at the methodology level, not the identity level.
- **Perceived Likelihood**: No proof in this section. It's pure philosophy. One concrete example or result would anchor the manifesto in reality.

**Energy/Flow:**
- The opening four lines ("Todo mundo está construindo AI. Chatbots. Automações. Agentes. Dashboards.") are strong.
- Energy drops in the chess paragraph — it's 3 sentences of extended metaphor that the reader has to unpack. The mapping from chess → business isn't immediate enough.
- "Chamamos isso de abordagem Revenue-First" feels like a branding moment forced into a manifesto. The name-drop interrupts the philosophical flow.

**PT-BR Issues:**
- "É a base de tudo que a Reis IA faz — seja implementando sistemas de AI na sua empresa, formando agências pra vender serviços de AI, ou construindo as parcerias que escalam tudo isso" — this is the subheadline repeated in different words. Same structure: listing all three pillars in one sentence. Same problem: too much information compressed.

**Paragraph Length:**
- The chess paragraph and the Revenue-First explanation paragraph are both 2+ lines. In a manifesto section with generous whitespace, this works visually — but the rhythm could be tighter.

**Closing Line:**
- "Esse é o único tipo que construímos." — Reviewer flagged this as functional but not memorable. Correct diagnosis. The two bolded lines above it ("AI pelo AI é experimento / AI pra receita é estratégia de negócio") are the real closer. The actual last line deflates the energy.

---

### 3. PILLAR CARDS

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Builder Card**: No specific timeframe for results. "Seu primeiro projeto entregue" — when? The Hormozi time-delay element is missing. Adding "nos primeiros 60 dias" or similar would dramatically increase perceived value.
- **Systems Card**: "done-for-you" is English jargon breaking PT-BR flow (reviewer flagged). "Garantia de performance de 60 dias" is buried in the proof anchor instead of being a headline-level differentiator.
- **Partners Card**: The weakest card. "Receita compartilhada, no modelo que faz sentido pra você" is vague — what model? What percentage range? What revenue potential?
- **Network Card**: "Eventos ao vivo e workshops práticos" is generic. Could be any community. No differentiator.

**Status Angle Missing:**
- None of the cards paint the picture of who the reader becomes. "Construa o Seu Negócio de AI" is about the action, not the identity transformation. Compare: "Seja a agência que vende AI — enquanto as outras ainda tentam entender."

**Specificity Gaps:**
- Builder: No cohort size, no success metrics, no timeline
- Systems: No implementation timeline, no average revenue impact
- Partners: No revenue-share range, no number of current partners
- Network: No member count, no event frequency

**Energy:**
- Cards are structurally solid but uniform in tone. Each card should have its own emotional register as noted in the briefing (Builder = aspirational, Systems = professional, Partners = opportunity, Network = inclusive). Currently they all read at the same intensity.

### 4. STATS

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Perceived Likelihood**: Reviewer flagged — no source attribution. For C-level readers, unsourced stats reduce credibility rather than build it.
- **Dream Outcome**: The stats frame the market problem well but don't explicitly connect to what Reis IA delivers. The transition from "91% report revenue increase" to "and Reis IA is how you get there" is missing.

**Energy/Flow:**
- The headline "Os dados são claros. Quando AI começa pela receita, funciona." is good but the second line is too explanatory. It tells the reader what to think instead of letting the stats do that work.
- Stat labels are functional but read like captions, not copy. "Esse é o Cemitério de Protótipos" is the strongest label — the others should match this energy.

**Missed Hourglass Opportunity:**
- The stats section doesn't leverage time at all. "74% alcançam ROI no primeiro ano" — the time element is there but not framed through the hourglass lens. "No primeiro ano" could be reframed as a time-recovery narrative.

---

### 5. FOUNDER / MORONI

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Dream Outcome**: The section explains Moroni's journey but doesn't paint the reader's future. It ends with "A Reis IA existe pra fechar esse gap" — gap between what and what? The dream outcome is implied, not stated.
- **Status**: Missing entirely. Moroni is positioned as a peer, which is correct — but there's no reflection of how working with him elevates the reader's own positioning.

**Energy/Flow:**
- Strong opening and middle. The time/hourglass paragraph ("Tem um recurso que nenhum sistema de AI fabrica...") is the longest paragraph in the section and comes right before the close. It creates a momentum wall when the reader should be accelerating toward the CTA.
- "perda composta" is a strong concept but not fully developed. One concrete example of compounding loss would make it visceral.

**PT-BR Issues:**
- "funnels, lançamentos, sistemas de aquisição de clientes pra empresas no Brasil e fora" — "funnels" is English. While the audience knows it, using it in the third word of Moroni's bio creates a disconnect with the PT-BR-first approach. "funis" exists and works.

**Premium Tone Drops:**
- "Empresas jogavam dinheiro em projetos de AI sem conexão nenhuma com receita" — "jogavam" + "conexão nenhuma" is strong PT-BR but the sentence is too long. Breaking it into two would maintain the visceral impact without diluting it.

### 6. OFFER PATHS

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Effort/Sacrifice**: Card A (Agência) mentions "sair do zero e chegar na primeira venda" but doesn't minimize perceived effort. How hard is it? How much time? Card B (Empresa) is better with the three pain fragments but the CTA is purely functional.
- **Time Delay**: Neither card gives a timeframe. "First sale" — when? "Revenue Audit" — how long until results?

**Energy/Flow:**
- The section headline "Uma operação. O ponto de entrada certo pro momento em que você está." is weak. It's descriptive, not motivating. It should create urgency for self-selection, not just describe the architecture.
- The Network catch-all "Não se encaixa em nenhum dos dois ainda?" has a slightly defeatist tone. It should feel like a valued option, not a consolation prize.

### 7. NEWSLETTER

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Dream Outcome**: "Receba o framework que nossos clientes pagam pra ter" is strong but could be more specific about what they'll be able to DO after reading.
- **Perceived Likelihood**: No social proof (subscriber count, past issues, specific results from content).

**Specificity:**
- "Insights Revenue-First, estudos de caso reais e as estratégias de AI que estamos usando agora" — what kind of case studies? What results? One specific example would outperform this list.

---

### 8. FOOTER CTA

**Hormozi Gaps:**
- **Status**: Reviewer's key recommendation. The footer focuses entirely on loss aversion (competitors acting, time passing) but never paints the positive status picture. The reader needs to see both what they lose by waiting AND what they become by acting.

**Energy/Flow:**
- The headline is one of the strongest in the entire page. "O custo real de esperar não é dinheiro. É tempo." — perfect.
- The subheadline is solid but could be tighter. "Cada sistema de AI que eles implementam este mês trabalha enquanto você ainda decide" — "este mês" is oddly specific and dates the copy. Remove or generalize.
- Microcopy: "A única decisão que o seu eu de daqui a um ano vai agradecer" — this is the status line. It should be elevated from microcopy to a more prominent position.

## CROSS-CUTTING GAPS

### 1. Status Angle (Hormozi #3) — SYSTEMIC WEAKNESS
The reviewer scored this as the #1 improvement area. Status language appears only in:
- Builder proof anchor (one line)
- Implicitly in chess metaphor

Missing from: Hero, Manifesto closing, Systems card, Founder section, Footer. The reader never clearly sees who they BECOME. This is the biggest gap between 8.7 and 9.5.

### 2. Translated-English Phrasing
- "done-for-you" (Systems card)
- "funnels" (Founder section)
- Subheadline structure mirrors English compound sentences
- "Revenue-First Framework" is acceptable as brand terminology but surrounding sentences sometimes read like English-first thinking translated to PT-BR

### 3. Subheadline Density
Flagged by CMO and reviewer. 41 words. Should be 20 or fewer. The pillar enumeration belongs in the Pillar Cards section, not the hero.

### 4. Missing Specificity Where It Matters Most
- No implementation timelines anywhere
- No client results (even anonymized)
- No cohort numbers for Builder
- No "X companies served" or equivalent social proof
- Stats section has numbers but they're industry-wide, not Reis IA-specific

### 5. Hourglass Narrative Fragmented
The time/hourglass motif appears in three places but never builds on itself. Each mention feels standalone. A thread that connects them — time in the hero → time in the manifesto → time in founder → time in footer — would create a subliminal narrative arc.

### 6. Section Transitions
No transitional elements between sections. The page reads as 8 independent modules. A brief narrative bridge (even one sentence) between key sections would create story-like flow.
