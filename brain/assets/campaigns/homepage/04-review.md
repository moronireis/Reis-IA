## PASS 1 — BRAND VOICE (Score: 9/10)

### Premium consultivo?
**YES.** The tone throughout reads as a strategic peer speaking to a C-level audience. Moroni is positioned as a strategist who figured something out, not a guru. The manifesto section in particular nails the Apple keynote register adapted to Brazilian Portuguese.

### PT-BR natural?
**YES.** Contractions ("pra", "pro"), oral expressions ("a gente trabalha", "jogar dinheiro fora", "no ralo", "ficava fazendo"), and sentence structures are authentically Brazilian executive. No forced formality, no academic Portuguese.

### Identity test — "Se eu cobrisse o nome, saberia que e a Reis IA?"
**YES.** The Revenue-First thesis, Prototype Graveyard enemy narrative, chess/hourglass motifs, and the specific "where is the revenue?" question create a voice that is unmistakably Reis IA. No other AI company in Brazil is saying this with this clarity.

### Issues:
| # | Severity | Section | Issue | Suggested Fix |
|---|----------|---------|-------|---------------|
| 1.1 | LOW | Card Systems (body) | "done-for-you" is English jargon that breaks the PT-BR flow. While the audience may know the term, it feels like a slip in a copy that otherwise avoids unnecessary anglicisms. | Consider "implementacao completa" or keep "done-for-you" in italics as a deliberate borrowed term. Not blocking — the audience likely understands it. |
| 1.2 | LOW | Tagline | "AI Revenue, na pratica" — mixing English and Portuguese in the tagline works because "Revenue-First" is the brand's methodology name. Acceptable. | No change needed. Noting for record. |

### Strengths:
- Hero headline ("AI nao e um projeto de tecnologia. E uma estrategia de receita.") is the single best line in the entire copy. Differentiating, quotable, passes the identity test perfectly.
- Founder section avoids LinkedIn bio energy. "Mais de uma decada em marketing digital — funnels, lancamentos, sistemas" reads like someone talking, not listing credentials.
- The brand never oversells. Tone stays at 0/10 sell intensity as briefed.

## PASS 2 — HORMOZI FRAMEWORK (Score: 8.5/10)

### Value Equation Present?
**YES.**
- **Maximize outcome**: Revenue generation is named explicitly across Hero, Manifesto, Stats, Founder, Footer.
- **Maximize likelihood**: 91% stat, 74% ROI stat, 67% vs 22% specialist advantage, Revenue-First methodology with a name.
- **Minimize time**: "30 minutos", "60 dias" guarantee on Systems, "primeiro ano" ROI, hourglass motif.
- **Minimize effort**: "done-for-you" (Systems), "sem contratar engenheiros" (Builder), "a gente constroi" positioning.

### 4 Angles Present?
| Angle | Present? | Where |
|-------|----------|-------|
| More Good | YES | Pillar cards (concrete deliverables), Stats (91%, 74%), Builder bullets |
| Less Bad | YES | Prototype Graveyard (42% stat), Manifesto enemy narrative, Founder "jogar dinheiro fora" |
| Status | PARTIAL | Implicit in chess metaphor and "quem passa pelo Builder ja esta jogando o proximo lance" but could be stronger |
| Social Risk | YES | Footer CTA ("seus concorrentes nao estao esperando"), Founder ("perda composta"), time urgency |

### Specificity?
**STRONG.** Numbers are present (42%, 91%, 74%, 67% vs 22%, 30 minutos, 60 dias). Concrete deliverables in bullet lists (AI sales agents, CRM integrations, WhatsApp). Specific scenarios ("leads que somem", "processos que nao escalam").

### Enemy Narrative?
**STRONG.** The Prototype Graveyard is clearly established as the enemy. The tech-first approach is named and attacked. "AI pelo AI e experimento" is a clean, quotable enemy declaration.

### Issues:
| # | Severity | Section | Issue | Suggested Fix |
|---|----------|---------|-------|---------------|
| 2.1 | MEDIUM | Status angle | The Status angle (Hormozi angle 3) is the weakest of the four. The copy focuses on what the visitor avoids and gains, but underdevelops how they'll be PERCEIVED after working with Reis IA. The chess metaphor touches it but doesn't close it. | Consider adding one status-forward line in the Offer Paths section or Founder CTA. Designer decision, not copy blocker. |

### Strengths:
- The Value Equation is embedded naturally, not mechanically. The reader feels the math without seeing it.
- The Prototype Graveyard works as both a data point (42%) and an emotional concept. Dual-function enemy.
- The 42% stat positioned "against" the other three is an excellent narrative tension device.

**YES.** The humanization log confirms all 9 occurrences of prohibited words were eliminated:
- "ecossistema" (6x removed)
- "transformar/transforma" (2x removed)

### No prohibited structures?
**YES.**
- No generic openings ("No mundo atual de...")
- No 5+ item lists with identical grammatical structure
- No paragraphs with 5 same-length sentences
- No conclusion that repeats the introduction

### Rhythm variety?
**YES.** The copy demonstrates strong rhythm variation:
- Short fragments: "Com precisao.", "Rapido. Com proposito.", "Sem montar time tecnico."
- Long explanatory sentences: Manifesto paragraph 4, Founder paragraph 3

- Questions: "onde isso gera dinheiro?", Footer subheadline rhetorical question

### Human signals?
**YES.** 14 contractions (pra/pro), 6 Brazilian oral expressions, 8 intentional fragments, 5 em-dash asides, 2 rhetorical questions. The humanization log is thorough and accurate.

### Read-aloud test?

## PASS 4 — STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT (Score: 8.5/10)

### Positioning page, not sales page?
**YES.** The copy never hard-sells. No pricing. No urgency manufactured from nothing. The emotional arc follows the briefing exactly: curiosity (Hero) --> recognition (Manifesto) --> orientation (Pillars) --> credibility (Stats) --> trust (Founder) --> self-selection (Paths) --> low-commitment (Newsletter) --> action (Footer).

### Routes visitors correctly?
**YES.** All CTAs map to real endpoints:
- /agendar: Hero primary, Systems card, Offer Path B, Footer
- /aplicar: Builder card, Offer Path A
- /builder: Founder dual CTA
- Newsletter/network: Network card, below Offer Paths, Newsletter section

No dead-end anchors. The briefing's concern about "zero working conversion mechanisms" in the current site is fully addressed.

### Mixed ICP — works for all segments?
**YES.** The Hero speaks to the universal frustration (AI not generating revenue). The Pillar Cards provide four distinct entry points. The Offer Paths create clean binary self-selection. The Network catches everyone not ready for either path.

### No SaaS patterns?
**YES.** Zero pricing tables, zero tier cards, zero feature comparison grids, zero "choose your plan" patterns. All CTAs route to /agendar or /aplicar. Confirmed.

### Chess motif placement?
**YES.** Present in Manifesto (paragraph 4, chess analogy) and Builder card proof anchor ("ja esta jogando o proximo lance"). Matches briefing specification.

### Hourglass motif placement?
**YES.** Present in Systems card proof anchor (time/60 days), Founder section (penultimate paragraph — "tempo" as non-recoverable resource), and Footer CTA (headline + motif). Matches briefing specification.

### Partners/Network "Em breve"?
**YES.** Both cards have soft CTAs with status notes indicating "Em breve" routing to interest forms or newsletter.

### Issues:
| # | Severity | Section | Issue | Suggested Fix |
|---|----------|---------|-------|---------------|
| 4.1 | MEDIUM | Subheadline | The subheadline is 41 words. The CMO briefing explicitly says: "The current subheadline is too long and tries to explain the entire ecosystem. Simplify." While the humanizer improved it (removed "ecossistema"), it's still a dense sentence that lists three things (implementa, forma, constroi parcerias) plus a closing question. It works — but it could be tighter. | Consider a two-sentence version: "A Reis IA faz AI gerar receita — pra empresas e agencias. Tudo comeca com uma pergunta: onde esta a receita?" Cuts to ~20 words, keeps the thesis, loses the pillar enumeration (which the Pillar Cards section handles anyway). Not blocking — current version is functional. |
| 4.2 | LOW | Section architecture | The briefing lists "Logo Marquee" as section 2 (with caveat: only if real logos). The copy skips it entirely without noting whether real logos exist. | Designer/dev decision, not copy issue. Note for handoff: Logo Marquee should be included ONLY if real client logos are available. If not, omit entirely per CMO briefing. |
| 4.3 | LOW | Headline Variation 1 | "Seu projeto de AI foi parar no cemiterio. Existe um caminho diferente." — the humanizer notes "usar em landing pages de remarketing, nao na homepage principal." This is good guidance but should be more explicitly marked as NOT for homepage use to avoid designer confusion. | Add a visible tag: **[NAO USAR NA HOMEPAGE — reservado para remarketing]** |

### Strengths:
- The page architecture follows the briefing's emotional journey spec precisely.
- Self-selection routing is clean and binary — exactly as briefed.
- The Newsletter section avoids the generic "Assine nossa newsletter" trap. "Receba o framework que nossos clientes pagam pra ter" is a strong value-first headline.
