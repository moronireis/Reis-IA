## 1. Strategic Role of the Homepage

The homepage is a **positioning page**, not a sales page. Its job is threefold:

1. **Establish the brand thesis** in under 5 seconds — "AI is a revenue strategy, not a technology project"
2. **Build enough trust** for the visitor to self-identify with a pillar
3. **Route each visitor** to the correct next step (pillar page or /agendar)

The homepage does NOT need to close anyone. It needs to make the visitor think: "These people understand my problem. I want to know more."

### What This Means for Copy
- No hard selling. No pricing. No urgency manufactured from nothing.
- Every section earns the right to the next scroll.
- The emotional arc is: **curiosity --> recognition --> trust --> self-selection --> action**.
- Confidence is the dominant emotion. Not hype, not fear. Grounded authority.

### Critical Context from Phase 1 Review
The current site has zero working conversion mechanisms. All CTAs are dead-end anchors. This briefing assumes that by the time this copy ships, Cal.com will be embedded at /agendar, and the Builder application form will exist at /aplicar. Copy must route to these real endpoints.

## 2. ICP Strategy for Homepage

### The Common Thread

All four ICPs share one thing: **they suspect AI should be making them money, but it is not.** Whether they are an agency owner who cannot sell AI, a CEO whose AI project died in prototype, an SMB drowning in manual work, or an AI professional who cannot monetize their skills — the frustration is the same.

The homepage speaks to this universal frustration: **the gap between the promise of AI and the reality of implementation**.

### Awareness Levels Entering the Homepage

| Traffic Source | Likely Awareness Level | What They Need to See First |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Paid ads (LinkedIn, Meta) | Solution-aware | Differentiation — why Reis IA, not alternatives |
| Organic search (Google) | Problem-aware to solution-aware | Clear naming of their pain, then the mechanism |
| YouTube/content referral | Product-aware | Proof and routing — they already know the brand |
| Direct/referral | Most aware | Fast path to action — /agendar or /aplicar |
| Social media (Instagram, LinkedIn posts) | Unaware to problem-aware | The big idea first — reframe how they think about AI |

**Implication**: The hero must work for ALL levels. The big idea ("AI is a revenue strategy") works because it reframes for the unaware, differentiates for the solution-aware, and confirms for the product-aware.

### Where Each ICP Self-Selects

| ICP Segment | Self-Selection Point | Route To |
|------------|---------------------|----------|
| Agency owners | Pillar card: Builder + Partners | /builder or /aplicar |
| CEOs / B2B companies ($5M-$50M) | Pillar card: Systems + Offer path "empresas" | /systems or /agendar |
| SMB owners | Pillar card: Systems + Offer path "empresas" | /systems or /agendar |
| AI professionals / entrepreneurs | Pillar card: Builder + Network | /builder or newsletter |

The **Offer Paths section** (Section 9) is the primary self-selection mechanism. Two clear paths:
- "Sou dono de agencia" --> Builder/Partners
- "Preciso de AI na minha empresa" --> Systems

This binary keeps it clean. Network absorbs everyone not ready for either path via the newsletter.

## 3. Hormozi 4-Angle Material (Adapted for Umbrella Positioning)

### Value Equation Applied to Homepage

```
Value = (Revenue from AI x Confidence it works) / (Time to results x Effort required)
```

The homepage must:
- **Maximize perceived outcome**: AI that generates revenue, not experiments
- **Maximize perceived likelihood**: Specialist track record, Revenue-First methodology, stats
- **Minimize perceived time**: Speed of implementation, quick wins, structured process
- **Minimize perceived effort**: "We do the work" (Systems) or "We guide you" (Builder), not "figure it out"

### Angle 1: More Good (What the ecosystem delivers)

**For the homepage umbrella — not pillar-specific:**
- AI implementations that connect directly to revenue (not "innovation" or "digital transformation")
- A structured methodology that has a name and a framework (Revenue-First) — not ad hoc consulting
- An ecosystem that scales with you: start with one project, grow into partnership
- Access to a community and network of practitioners who are doing this, not just talking about it
- The clarity of knowing EXACTLY where AI makes money in your specific business (the Audit)

**Specific material for the copywriter:**
- 91% of SMBs with AI report revenue increases (when done right)
- 74% achieve ROI within year one with specialist implementation
- 67% success rate with specialists vs. 22% with generalists — 3x difference
- Revenue-First Framework: strategy before technology, revenue before features
- Tangible deliverables: AI sales agents, support systems, lead qualification, operational automation

### Angle 2: Less Bad (What they avoid)

**Universal pains across all ICPs:**
- The Prototype Graveyard: 42% of companies abandoned AI projects in 2025
- Wasted investment: companies spending R$50K-R$500K on AI with no measurable ROI
- Confidence crisis: buyer confidence in AI agents dropped from 43% to 22% in one year
- Gartner projection: 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027
- The "tech-first trap": starting with what is technically possible instead of what is financially valuable
- Death by generalist: hiring agencies that say yes to everything and deliver on nothing

**Emotional translation for the copywriter:**
- "Voce ja investiu em AI e nao viu resultado? Voce nao esta sozinho. 42% das empresas abandonaram seus projetos."
- "O problema nunca foi a tecnologia. Foi a pergunta errada no inicio."
- "Cada mes sem estrategia de AI e mais um mes alimentando o Cemiterio de Prototipos."

### Angle 3: Status (How being part of the ecosystem positions the client)

**For the decision-maker personally:**
- Being the CEO who turned AI from cost center into revenue engine
- Being the agency owner who saw the AI wave early and positioned before competitors
- Being the founder who stopped reacting to AI trends and started playing strategically
- Moving from "we should do something about AI" to "our AI systems generated X in revenue last quarter"

**Chess metaphor application:**
- Strategic positioning, not reactive scrambling
- Playing the long game while competitors chase shiny tools
- Every move intentional — the Revenue-First methodology as the chess strategy

**For the copywriter — status language:**
- "Enquanto outros experimentam, voce executa."
- "Ser o lider que transformou AI de projeto de TI em estrategia de receita."
- "A diferenca entre quem reage a AI e quem posiciona com AI."

### Angle 4: Social Risk (What happens if they do nothing)

**The cost of inaction — escalating timeline:**
- **Now**: Competitors are evaluating and starting AI implementation
- **6 months**: Early movers have working systems, compounding data advantage
- **12 months**: The gap between AI-implemented and non-AI businesses becomes visible in revenue
- **24 months**: Bottom 60-70% of agencies face commoditization; non-AI businesses lose structural competitiveness

**Hourglass metaphor application:**
- Time is the one resource AI cannot manufacture and money cannot buy back
- Every week without the right AI strategy is a week of compounding opportunity cost
- The window is open now — but windows close

**For the copywriter — social risk language:**
- "Seus concorrentes nao estao esperando o momento perfeito."
- "A distancia entre quem agiu e quem nao agiu so aumenta com o tempo."
- "O custo real de esperar nao e dinheiro. E tempo."

## 4. Creative Direction

### How Homepage Tone Differs from Pillar Pages

| Element | Homepage | Builder Page | Systems Page |
|---------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Tone | Premium umbrella, grounded confidence | Energetic, aspirational, community | Professional, results-driven, corporate-safe |
| Sell intensity | 0/10 — pure positioning | 7/10 — application funnel | 6/10 — consultation funnel |
| Specificity | Broad — speaks to all ICPs | Narrow — agencies and entrepreneurs | Narrow — CEOs and operations leaders |
| Primary emotion | "These people get it" | "I want to be part of this" | "They can solve my problem" |
| CTA energy | Soft routing | Application urgency | Consultation invitation |

### The Emotional Journey (Section by Section)

```
HERO              --> CURIOSITY: "AI is not a tech project? Tell me more."
MANIFESTO         --> RECOGNITION: "This is exactly what I've been thinking."
PILLAR CARDS      --> ORIENTATION: "Oh, they do multiple things. Which one is for me?"
STATS             --> CREDIBILITY: "The data backs this up."
FOUNDER STORY     --> TRUST: "This person has a real perspective, not just marketing."
OFFER PATHS       --> SELF-SELECTION: "I know which path is mine."
NEWSLETTER        --> LOW-COMMITMENT: "I'm not ready yet, but I want to stay close."
FOOTER CTA        --> ACTION: "OK, I should at least start a conversation."
```

### Visual/Structural Notes for Designer

1. **Hero**: Maximum visual impact. Two-line headline in large type. Dark background. The brand thesis must be readable in under 2 seconds. Dual CTA buttons with clear visual hierarchy (primary = filled, secondary = ghost/outline).

2. **Logo Marquee**: Keep if we have real logos. REMOVE if still placeholder. Placeholder logos destroy credibility. Better to have nothing than fake social proof.

3. **Manifesto**: Full-width. Generous whitespace. Let the copy breathe. Chess motif integrated here — subtle knight icon or chess piece visual element. This section should feel like a manifesto, not a sales pitch.

4. **Pillar Cards**: Four cards, equal weight visually. Builder and Systems get active CTAs. Partners gets "Em breve" or waitlist CTA. Network gets soft CTA. Chess icon on Builder card. Hourglass icon on Systems card.

5. **Stats**: Four numbers, large type, with context labels below each. Not just "91%" but "91% das empresas que implementaram AI corretamente reportam aumento de receita." The context transforms a number into a story.

6. **Founder Story**: Photo of Moroni (real photo — placeholder destroys this section). Text alongside, not above/below. Peer positioning: Moroni speaks as a strategist who figured something out, not a guru dispensing wisdom. Hourglass motif integrated into the time/compounding message.

7. **Offer Paths**: Two clear cards side by side. Left = "Sou dono de agencia" (routes to Builder). Right = "Preciso de AI na minha empresa" (routes to Systems). Below both: soft Network CTA for people not ready for either.

8. **Newsletter**: Simple. One-field email capture. Strong headline about what they get (not "Subscribe to our newsletter"). Value-first: "Receba o framework que usamos" or similar.

9. **Footer CTA**: Full-width dark section. Hourglass motif. The urgency here is philosophical (time is passing, competitors are acting) not manufactured (fake countdown timers). Single CTA to /agendar.

### Sections to REMOVE or HIDE (Based on Phase 1 Review)

- **Interactive Demo section**: Remove entirely until real demos exist. Empty demo section hurts credibility.
- **Community section with placeholder avatars**: Remove until real community members can be shown.
- **Any "coming soon" badges on Partners/Network**: Reframe as "Em breve" with waitlist/interest capture, or reduce visual prominence.
- **Highlight Banner (next Builder cohort)**: Keep ONLY if there is a real date. Remove if placeholder.

## 5. Deliverables for the Copywriter

All deliverables below must be written in **Brazilian Portuguese (PT-BR)**. Follow the brand voice rules in `.claude/rules/brand-voice.md`, the Hormozi framework in `.claude/rules/hormozi-framework.md`, and the humanization rules in `.claude/rules/humanization-rules.md`.

### General Copy Rules

- All CTAs route to: `/agendar`, `/aplicar`, `/builder`, `/systems`, or newsletter signup
- No pricing on the homepage. Zero.
- No SaaS patterns (no tier cards, no feature comparison tables, no "choose your plan")
- Chess motif appears in: Manifesto section, Builder pillar card
- Hourglass motif appears in: Systems pillar card proof anchor, Founder section closing, Footer CTA
- Every section must pass the brand voice test: "Se eu cobrisse o nome da empresa, saberia que e a Reis IA?"
- Humanization checklist must be applied to every paragraph before final delivery

### Deliverable List

#### 5.1 — Hero Section

**Headline principal + 3 variacoes**

The primary headline must communicate the brand thesis in two short lines. Maximum impact, minimum words. The current headline ("AI Nao E um Projeto de Tecnologia. E uma Estrategia de Revenue.") works but needs refinement:
- "Revenue" is English — evaluate whether to keep it (the audience uses it) or translate
- Test a version with the chess metaphor
- Test a version that leads with the enemy (Prototype Graveyard)
- Test a version that leads with the benefit (revenue generation)

Requirements:
- 2 lines max per headline
- Works at 48px+ font size
- Immediately differentiating — cannot apply to any other AI company

**Subheadline**

Expands the thesis. Answers: "Who is Reis IA, who is it for, and what makes it different?" in 1-2 sentences. The current subheadline is too long and tries to explain the entire ecosystem. Simplify.

**Hero body copy** (1-2 short paragraphs)

Optional — only if needed. The hero might be stronger with just headline + subheadline + CTA. If body copy is included, it should answer: "Why should I care about this right now?" Keep under 40 words.

**Hero CTAs**

- Primary: Routes to /agendar — "Agende uma conversa" or variant. Warm, not aggressive.
- Secondary: Routes down-page (anchor to pillar cards or offer paths) — "Veja como funciona" or variant.
- Microcopy below CTAs: One line that removes friction. "30 minutos. Sem compromisso. Um mapa claro de onde AI gera receita no seu negocio."

#### 5.2 — Manifesto Section ("O Que Acreditamos")

This section is the philosophical core of the homepage. It establishes WHY Reis IA exists and WHAT it stands against.

Requirements:
- Keep the "Revenue-First" philosophy as the anchor
- Integrate the chess metaphor naturally (strategy before action, positioning before technology)
- Name the enemy: the Prototype Graveyard, tech-first thinking, AI pelo AI
- End with a clear declaration line that is quotable and shareable
- The existing copy in `website-main.md` Section 2 is strong. Refine, do not rewrite from zero.
- Apply humanization: vary sentence length, add Brazilian executive tone markers, avoid AI-sounding patterns
- Keep the manifesto under 200 words. Every word must earn its place.

#### 5.3 — Pillar Cards (4 cards)

Each card needs:
- **Title**: Clear, benefit-oriented (not just the pillar name)
- **Body**: 2-3 sentences explaining what this pillar does and for whom
- **Feature list**: 3-4 bullet points — concrete, specific deliverables (not vague benefits)
- **Who it's for**: One line identifying the ICP
- **CTA**: Active link text
- **Proof anchor**: One line of credibility below the CTA

Card-specific direction:

**Builder Card:**
- Lead angle: "Build and sell AI services without being technical"
- Chess motif in the headline or proof anchor
- CTA: "Aplicar para o Builder -->" routes to /aplicar
- ICP: Agency owners, entrepreneurs
- Tone: Aspirational, energetic

**Systems Card:**
- Lead angle: "AI implementation that generates measurable revenue"
- Hourglass motif in the proof anchor (time saved, time returned)
- CTA: "Agendar um AI Revenue Audit -->" routes to /agendar
- ICP: CEOs, operations leaders, business owners
- Tone: Professional, results-focused

**Partners Card:**
- Lead angle: "Sell AI without building it — we do the implementation"
- Status: "Em breve" or soft waitlist CTA
- CTA: "Quero saber mais -->" routes to a simple interest form or anchor
- ICP: Agencies ready for partnership
- Tone: Opportunity, low commitment

**Network Card:**
- Lead angle: "Community, events, and education for AI practitioners"
- Status: "Em breve" or soft CTA
- CTA: "Entrar na comunidade -->" routes to newsletter or community signup
- ICP: All segments — the catch-all
- Tone: Inclusive, community-focused

#### 5.4 — Stats Section

**Section headline**: Frame the stats as proof that the Revenue-First approach works when done right — contrasted against the industry failure rate.

**4 stats with context labels:**

| Stat | Context | Source |
|------|---------|--------|
| 91% | das empresas que implementaram AI corretamente reportam aumento de receita | SMB research |
| 74% | alcancam ROI no primeiro ano com implementacao especializada | Market data |
| 67% | taxa de sucesso com especialistas vs 22% com generalistas | Implementation research |
| 42% | das empresas abandonaram projetos de AI em 2025 | Gartner/industry |

The 42% stat should be positioned as "the problem" while the other three are "the solution." Create narrative tension between the two.

#### 5.5 — Founder/Moroni Section

This is NOT a bio. It is credibility positioning. Moroni should come across as a **peer** — a strategist who figured something out and built a system around it.

Requirements:
- Lead with the insight, not the credentials
- The Revenue-First origin story: "I kept asking the same question — where is the revenue?"
- Mention experience in digital marketing, launches, funnels — but as context, not resume
- Hourglass motif in the closing: time wasted on wrong approaches, time saved by Revenue-First
- End with a soft dual CTA (agendar + builder)
- Keep under 150 words. Tight. Every sentence moves the reader forward.
- Refer to existing copy in `website-main.md` Section 6 — refine, do not rewrite from zero.

#### 5.6 — Offer Paths Section

Two cards side by side. This is the self-selection point.

**Card 1: "Sou dono de agencia"** (or similar identifier)
- Routes to Builder
- 2-3 lines describing what Builder delivers for agencies
- CTA: "Aplicar para o Builder -->" to /aplicar
- Do NOT include pricing

**Card 2: "Preciso de AI implementada no meu negocio"** (or similar identifier)
- Routes to Systems
- 2-3 lines describing what Systems delivers for companies
- CTA: "Agendar um AI Revenue Audit -->" to /agendar
- Do NOT include pricing

**Below both cards:**
- Soft Network CTA: "Ainda nao esta pronto? Faca parte da comunidade Reis IA." routes to newsletter or /network

#### 5.7 — Newsletter Section

- **Headline**: Value-driven, not "Assine nossa newsletter"
- **Description**: 1-2 lines explaining what they receive (framework insights, case studies, Revenue-First strategies)
- **Form copy**: Placeholder text for email field + button text

Suggested angle: Position the newsletter as receiving the same Revenue-First insights that clients pay for.

#### 5.8 — Footer CTA Section

The final push. Hourglass/time motif. Philosophical urgency, not manufactured pressure.

Requirements:
- **Headline**: About time and competition, not about Reis IA
- **Subheadline**: 1-2 lines expanding on why NOW matters
- **CTA**: Single button to /agendar
- **Microcopy**: Remove friction — "30 minutos, sem compromisso, mapa claro de oportunidades"
- Refer to existing variations in `website-main.md` Section 8 — select the strongest or refine

#### 5.9 — Meta Tags

- **Title tag**: Under 60 characters. Include "Reis IA" + key positioning phrase
- **Meta description**: Under 155 characters. Communicate what Reis IA does + for whom + differentiator
- **OG title**: Can be slightly different from title tag — more hook-oriented
- **OG description**: Under 200 characters. Optimized for social sharing

## 7. Copy Quality Gates

Before the copywriter delivers final copy, every section must pass:

1. **Brand voice test**: "Se eu cobrisse o nome, saberia que e a Reis IA?" (from brand-voice.md)
2. **Hormozi value equation**: Does this section increase perceived outcome/likelihood or decrease perceived time/effort?
3. **Humanization checklist**: Read aloud — does it sound like a Brazilian executive wrote it, not a chatbot? (from humanization-rules.md)
4. **Specificity check**: Are there numbers, scenarios, or concrete examples instead of vague benefits?
5. **CTA integrity**: Does every CTA route to a real, working endpoint? No dead-end anchors.
6. **No prohibited patterns**: No SaaS pricing tables, no tier cards, no "revolucionario/inovador/solucao robusta", no 3+ chained adjectives.
