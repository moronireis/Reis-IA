## PASS 1 — BRAND VOICE (Score: 9.5/10)

### What improved from v1.0 (was 9.0):
- **Anglicisms removed**: "done-for-you" → "completa", "funnels" → "funis", "gap" → "distancia". The copy now reads fully PT-BR native with only intentional English kept (Revenue-First as brand terminology, ROI as universally used by the audience).
- **Subheadline density fixed**: 41 → 18 words. No longer reads like a translated mission statement. Reads like a confident declaration.
- **Identity test stronger**: 9 status points distributed throughout create a more distinctive voice. The line "AI que Aparece no Faturamento" is unmistakably Reis IA — no other AI company in Brazil uses "faturamento" as the frame.

### What keeps it from 10.0:
- The Manifesto section, while strong, is a philosophy piece that any sophisticated AI strategist could write. It's differentiated by the Revenue-First thesis but not by a uniquely Reis IA voice element.
- Card Partners ("Venda AI. A Gente Constroi.") is clean but the simplest card — it could benefit from one more personality marker in the body.

## PASS 3 — HUMANIZATION (Score: 9.5/10)

### What improved from v1.0 (was 9.0):

**Anglicisms eliminated (3 fixes):**
- "done-for-you" → "completa"
- "funnels" → "funis"
- "gap" → "distancia"
These were subtle but detectable as English-first thinking. v2.0 reads as PT-BR written, not translated.

**New human signals added:**
- "nao como hobby" (Builder ICP) — aside that filters and feels like a real person talking
- "quem esta fazendo — nao so quem esta falando" (Network) — conversational contrast
- "Quer ficar perto antes de decidir?" (Network CTA) — warm question replacing exclusionary framing
- "Suas informacoes ficam com a gente" (newsletter privacy) — more personal than "nao sao compartilhadas"

**Rhythm maintained and improved:**
- Subheadline now fragments naturally: thesis + open loop
- Builder card body: 3 sentences → long / short / short with timeline as fragment
- Footer microcopy: short / medium / short with status punch

**Read-aloud test v2.0:**
- Hero: Cleaner flow — no density wall at subheadline
- Manifesto: Closing now has weight — "um numero no faturamento que justifica cada real investido" sounds like a CFO thinking out loud
- Pillar cards: Each has distinct energy as briefed (Builder aspirational, Systems professional, Partners clean, Network inclusive)
- Founder: "Ate entender por que." as headline addition pulls reader into the section
- Footer: Status close reads naturally — "ser visto como o lider que fez a coisa certa na hora certa" is something a board member would actually say

### What keeps it from 10.0:
- Stats section labels, while improved, are still the most "written" section — they read more like editorial copy than speech. This is partially inherent to the format (data context labels require precision).
- Newsletter description could use one more imperfection marker — it's clean but could feel slightly more casual.

## REMAINING OPPORTUNITIES (for future iterations)

These are not gaps — they are opportunities for when business conditions change:

1. **Reis IA-specific proof**: When first case studies are generated (per business-profile.md: "initial case studies being generated"), replace or supplement industry stats with proprietary data. This is the single biggest lever to push from 9.5 to 9.8+.

2. **Partners card specificity**: When the Partners program launches, add revenue-share model specifics, partner count, and success metrics.

3. **Network social proof**: When community hits meaningful numbers, add member count and event frequency.

4. **Logo Marquee**: Add when real client logos are available. Omit entirely until then (per CMO briefing).

5. **Manifesto voice**: Consider adding one "imperfection" — a parenthetical aside or self-correction — to make it even more human. Current version is polished; a small crack would add authenticity.
