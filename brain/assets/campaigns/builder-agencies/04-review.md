### Pass 1: Brand Voice (9/10)

**Consultivo premium tone**: YES. The copy consistently reads as a strategic peer speaking to another operator. Moroni is positioned as someone who built the path, not a guru on stage. Lines like "Nada de guru. Nada de palco. Um operador que montou o playbook dividindo com quem vai executar" nail the peer positioning.

**PT-BR natural**: YES. The oral-formal register is consistent throughout — "pra/pro", "Olha", "Na prática", "Funciona assim:" all land naturally. No academicismo detected.

**C-level/agency owner appropriate**: YES. The language is operational, revenue-focused, and practical. Uses terms agency owners already use (retainer, pipeline, proposta, precificação).

**No AI hype, no vague promises**: YES. Every claim is anchored in numbers (R$25K-R$80K per project, 60-70% commoditization stat, 12-week timeframe). No "revolucionario", no "disruptivo", no vague transformation language.

**Identity test ("Se eu cobrisse o nome, saberia que é Reis IA?")**: YES. The chess motif, the Builder/Partners ecosystem references, the "Cemiterio de Prototipos" enemy narrative, Moroni's peer-operator positioning, and the specific program structure are all distinctly Reis IA. This copy could not be mistaken for a generic AI program.

**Minor note**: "desenhado" still appears twice (lines 442, 490). The humanizer reduced it from 8+ occurrences, which is a significant improvement, but one more substitution on line 490 (e.g., "montado" or "construído") would fully eliminate the pattern.

### Pass 2: Hormozi Framework (8/10)

**Value equation optimized**: YES.
- Resultado Desejado: Clear (new AI revenue line, R$25K-R$80K per project, first client during program)
- Probabilidade Percebida: Strong (guarantee, implementation support, Reis IA as technical backbone, specific methodology)
- Tempo: Minimized (12 weeks, offer by Week 2, first client during program)
- Esforço: Minimized (5-8h/week, templates provided, Reis IA handles technical delivery)

**All 4 angles covered**:
- More Good: YES — revenue numbers, Partners pathway, premium positioning, higher ticket pricing
- Less Bad: YES — no more pricing by guessing, no more implementation fear, no more commoditization spiral
- Status: YES — peers asking how you pivoted, clients seeing you as AI-forward partner, team energy shift
- Social Risk: YES — no more improvising answers to "do you do AI?", no more reputation risk on delivery

**Specificity > generality**: YES. The copy is relentlessly specific — R$25K-R$80K per project, 12 weeks, 5-8 hours/week, 12 agencies per cohort, R$16K/R$32K/R$48K tiers, 67% vs 22% specialist success rate.

**Cemiterio de Prototipos / commoditization enemy**: PARTIALLY. The commoditization narrative is strong and present throughout (60-70% stat, "corrida pro fundo", competitive anxiety). However, the "Cemiterio de Prototipos" label — the named enemy from the Hormozi framework — does not appear explicitly in the copy. The concept is there (agencies trying AI and failing), but the named framing is absent. This is a MINOR issue because the agency ICP's enemy is more accurately "commoditization" than "prototype graveyard" (which maps better to the CEO ICP), so the strategic choice to not force the label is defensible.

**WHO x WHEN explored**: YES. The copy covers self (revenue now, 90 days, 1 year), team (morale, new roles), clients (upsell, retention), market/competitors (first mover advantage, cases accumulating), and peers (authority positioning). Temporal progression is woven into the Problem section, Guarantee, Final CTA, and email sequence.

**Deduction rationale**: -1 for the Cemiterio de Prototipos label absence (minor, defensible). -1 because the Status angle (Angle 3) could be slightly stronger in the main body copy — it appears mostly in the benefit blocks and "Para Quem E" section but is lighter in the Problem/Agitation and Solution sections where agency owners are most emotionally engaged.

### Pass 4: Strategic Alignment (9/10)

**Targets agency owners (NOT CEOs)**: YES. The entire copy speaks to "donos de agencia", uses operational language (vender, entregar, faturar, cliente, projeto, retainer), and frames the value as revenue + capability. No strategic leadership or "clarity and control" framing.

**Sells REVENUE + CAPABILITY (not clarity/control)**: YES. The core promise is consistently "new revenue line + ability to sell and deliver AI services." Every section ladders to revenue: R$25K-R$80K per project, first client during program, AI as 40-60% of agency revenue at 1 year.

**CTAs point to /aplicar or /agendar only**: CONFIRMED. All 13 CTAs across the page and email sequence point to /aplicar (primary) or /agendar (secondary). Zero dead-end anchors, zero self-serve checkout.

**No pricing tables/cards/SaaS patterns**: CONFIRMED. The Investment section (Entregavel extra, line 706) uses flowing text format with clear hierarchy. The Curso vs. Builder comparison table in the Solution section is a content table, not a pricing/SaaS pattern — this is appropriate and matches briefing guidance.

**Differentiated from CEO Builder campaign**: YES. The tone is more urgent and operational. The language is "vender", "entregar", "faturar" vs. "liderar", "posicionar". The proof points emphasize revenue numbers and peer success. The CTA energy is "Apply now — next cohort starts [DATE]" vs. composed "Apply when ready."

### Issues Found

**1. [MINOR] "desenhado" appears twice in active copy (lines 442, 490)**
Severity: MINOR
The humanizer reduced from 8+ to 2 occurrences. Line 490 ("O programa foi desenhado do jeito que...") could be varied to "montado" or "construído" for full pattern elimination.

**2. [MINOR] Cemiterio de Prototipos label absent**
Severity: MINOR
The concept is present (agencies trying and failing at AI), but the named enemy framing from the Hormozi framework does not appear. This is defensible for the agency ICP where "commoditization" is the more resonant enemy, but including the label once (perhaps in the Problem section) would strengthen the framework integration.

**3. [MINOR] Email sequence rhythm slightly uniform**
Severity: MINOR
Emails 2 and 3 have marginally more uniform paragraph rhythms compared to the main landing page copy. Adding one fragment or one-line paragraph to each would bring them to the same quality level.

**4. [MINOR] Status angle (Hormozi Angle 3) light in Problem/Solution sections**
Severity: MINOR
The status outcome — how peers, clients, and team see the agency owner after adding AI — is covered in Benefit Blocks and "Para Quem E" but is underrepresented in the high-emotion Problem/Agitation and Solution sections where it would amplify urgency.

**5. [MINOR] Investment tier descriptions could sharpen self-selection**
Severity: MINOR
The Group/Premium/VIP descriptions tell what's included but could be sharper about WHO each tier is for (agency size, AI readiness stage, speed of desired result) to help the reader match themselves faster.

### Recommended Changes

**1. Line 490 — replace "desenhado"**

- Recommended: "O programa foi montado do jeito que o Moroni teria querido ser mentorado"

**2. Problem section (around line 142) — consider adding Cemiterio de Prototipos label**
- After "Mais tutorial não resolve." consider: "O que a maioria das agências está fazendo agora é acumular protótipos internos de AI que nunca geraram um real — o Cemitério de Protótipos. O que resolve é outra coisa: um framework, uma metodologia e alguém que já construiu o caminho."
- Note: This is optional. If the CMO strategist made a deliberate decision to keep the label for the CEO campaign only, ignore this recommendation.

**3. Email 2 (line 619) — add rhythm break**

A favor de quem já se mexeu." [break] "Não estou dizendo isso pra criar pressão artificial. Os dados fazem isso sozinhos."
- This is already close; just ensure the email rendering preserves the line break for rhythm impact.

- Group: add "Ideal pra agências de 3-10 pessoas que estão dando os primeiros passos em AI como serviço."

### Strengths

1. **Relentless specificity.** Every claim is anchored in numbers, timeframes, or concrete scenarios. R$25K-R$80K per project, 12 weeks, 5-8 hours/week, 12 agencies per cohort, 60-70% commoditization stat, 67% vs 22% specialist success rate. This is textbook Hormozi execution.

2. **Problem/Agitation section is exceptional.** Lines 120-164 are the emotional core of the page and they deliver. The "Você viu" opening, the specific scenarios in the bullet list, the commoditization data, and the hourglass close ("O tempo não passa. Ele compõe — a favor delas.") work together seamlessly.

3. **Chess motif integration is natural, not forced.** "Um curso te dá as peças do tabuleiro. O Builder te ensina a jogar o jogo." and the module descriptions with chess-inspired headers ("O movimento de abertura", "Posição antes de avançar", "Execute com precisão", "Endgame") carry strategic meaning without feeling decorative.

4. **Humanization quality is high.** The oral-formal register is consistent. Fragments, parenthetical asides, self-corrections, and rhythm variation all feel natural. The copy reads as a person speaking, not as AI generating. The "read aloud" test passes convincingly.

5. **Guarantee section is masterful.** Lines 454-474 handle risk reversal with confidence, transparency about completion requirements, and the brilliant hourglass close: "Tempo. Se você esperar mais três meses antes de aplicar, nenhuma política de reembolso devolve esses três meses." This reframes the guarantee from "safety net" to "urgency driver" without feeling manipulative.

6. **Objection handling blocks are peer-level, not salesy.** Each block acknowledges the objection genuinely before reframing. "Olha, essa é uma preocupação legítima. E eu levo a sério." (line 436) is exactly the right tone for agency owners who have been burned before.

7. **Perfect CTA discipline.** All 13 CTAs across the page and email sequence point exclusively to /aplicar or /agendar. No SaaS patterns, no pricing cards, no self-serve checkout. Investment section uses text format as mandated.

8. **Email sequence stands alone.** Each email works independently (as briefed), with different angles (packaging problem, cost of waiting, ROI math) and different CTA routing (Email 1: /aplicar, Email 2: /agendar, Email 3: /aplicar).

9. **Clear differentiation from CEO campaign.** The entire copy speaks "revenue and capability" language, not "clarity and control." The urgency is operational, the proof is financial, and the CTA energy is appropriately more urgent than the CEO version would be.

## VERDICT SUMMARY

**APPROVE.** Overall score 8.5/10, no dimension below 8. All 5 identified issues are MINOR — none are blocking. The copy is ready for dev-agent implementation with the optional polish items above. The recommended changes would lift this from strong to exceptional but are not required for go-live.
