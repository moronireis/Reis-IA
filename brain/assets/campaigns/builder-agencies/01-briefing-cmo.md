## A) ICP Definition — Refined for This Campaign

### Decision Maker Profile

- **Title**: Agency owner or co-founder
- **Age**: 28-45
- **Agency size**: 3-20 people, R$1M-R$15M revenue
- **Services today**: Digital marketing, web development, design, social media, performance marketing, or a combination
- **Business model**: Retainer-based or project-based client services
- **Geography**: Brazil (primary), Portuguese-speaking markets
- **Technical comfort**: Uses tools daily (Make, Zapier, CRMs, analytics). NOT a developer. Comfortable learning new tools but does not write code.
- **Decision style**: Practical operator. Evaluates investments by ROI speed, not theoretical elegance. Wants to see the path from investment to first revenue before committing.
- **Time availability**: Running a full agency. Has 5-8 hours/week available for growth work, not more.

### Awareness Level

**Solution-aware.** They know they need AI capabilities. They are actively evaluating options:
1. Learn it themselves (courses, YouTube, trial-and-error)
2. Hire an AI developer (expensive, risky, slow)
3. Partner with an AI company (what Reis IA offers)
4. Use no-code tools and figure it out (limited, no methodology)

They have NOT found a structured path that combines mentorship + implementation support + partnership pathway. That gap is our entry point.

### Primary Pains (ordered by emotional intensity)

1. **Commoditization fear**: "My agency is becoming interchangeable. Clients negotiate harder every quarter. If I don't differentiate, I'm dead in 2-3 years."
2. **Competitive anxiety**: "Competitors are already selling AI. I saw one rebrand around AI services and raise their prices. I'm losing ground every month I wait."
3. **Revenue plateau**: "We've been at the same revenue range for 12-18 months. I need a new lever. AI looks like the biggest one since social media."
4. **Packaging paralysis**: "I know AI is the opportunity. I just don't know what to sell, how to price it, how to deliver it without embarrassing myself."
5. **Implementation fear**: "What if I sell AI and the implementation fails? My client blames me. My reputation is on the line."

### Secondary Pains

6. **Time scarcity**: "I'm already working 50+ hours/week running the agency. Where do I find time to learn a new service line?"
7. **Past burned investments**: "I've bought courses and programs before. Some were good, most didn't produce revenue. I'm skeptical of anything promising transformation."
8. **Technical imposter syndrome**: "Can I really sell AI if I can't build it myself? Won't clients see through me?"

### What They've Tried (and Why It Didn't Work)

| What they tried | Why it failed |
|----------------|--------------|
| YouTube tutorials / free content | Overwhelming volume, no structure, no accountability, no methodology for packaging into a service |
| ChatGPT / Claude experiments | Interesting but no bridge from "playing with AI" to "selling AI services for R$15K-R$80K" |
| Make.com / n8n / Zapier automations | Hit complexity ceiling fast. Can build simple flows but not production-grade systems clients pay premium for |
| Hired a freelance developer | Expensive, communication gap between business need and technical delivery, no methodology transfer |
| "We do AI" pitch to existing client | Scrambled to deliver, underpriced, scope creep, no repeatable process |
| Generic AI course | Theory-heavy, no implementation support, no offer creation, no client acquisition system |

### Buying Triggers (what makes them act NOW)

1. Lost a client to a competitor offering AI services
2. Client explicitly asked "do you do AI?" and they had to say no (or bluff)
3. Saw a peer agency launch AI services and visibly grow
4. Revenue plateau hitting 3+ consecutive months
5. Attended an AI event or consumed a piece of content that made the urgency visceral
6. A team member brought up AI and they realized they had no strategy
7. End-of-quarter revenue review — looking for the next growth lever

### Objections (ranked by frequency)

1. "Can I really sell AI if I'm not technical?" — highest frequency, rooted in imposter syndrome
2. "Is R$16K-R$60K worth it? What if it doesn't work for my agency?" — price anxiety masked as ROI question
3. "I don't have time while running my agency" — legitimate concern, often also a fear excuse
4. "What if I don't get clients during the program?" — wants certainty before commitment
5. "I've invested in programs before and didn't get results" — trust deficit from past burns

## D) Creative Direction

### Tone — Specific to Agency Owners

The CEO version of Builder copy speaks to CLARITY and CONTROL — the executive learns to lead AI strategy from a position of authority.

The Agency version speaks to REVENUE and CAPABILITY — the agency owner gets a concrete new revenue stream without hiring engineers.

**Tone calibration for agencies**:
- More operational and direct than CEO version. Less philosophical.
- Agency owners are pragmatic operators. They want: what is it, what do I get, how fast, how much, what's the risk.
- Consultative premium (Apple, Porsche, Stripe aesthetic) but with practitioner energy — like a peer who already figured it out sharing the playbook, not a consultant lecturing from a stage.
- Confident without arrogance. Moroni speaks as a peer who built this path, not as a guru who has all the answers.
- Urgency grounded in market data, not hype. The numbers make the case — the copy presents them without screaming.

### Language Rules (PT-BR)

- Brazilian Portuguese, executive register
- Avoid unnecessary anglicisms. Use English terms (ROI, framework, SOP) only when the audience already uses them naturally
- Zero academicism: no "outrossim", "destarte", "no que tange"
- No AI hype words: "revolucionario", "disruptivo", "o futuro e agora", "solucao robusta"
- Humanization rules apply fully — see `/Users/moronireis/Projetos vscode/.claude/rules/humanization-rules.md`
- Rhythm: vary sentence length. Short. Then a longer sentence that builds the argument. Then short again. Fragments when impact demands it.
- Brazilian executive oral-formal register: "Na pratica", "Funciona assim:", "Olha" (sparingly)
- Parenthetical asides for personality: (como quem ja passou por isso)
- No emojis in any copy

### What NOT to Say

1. **No SaaS language**: No "planos", "tiers" as visual cards, no toggle pricing, no feature comparison grids. Tiers can be mentioned in text format — but never as a pricing table UI component.
2. **No tech jargon without business context**: Never mention APIs, code, neural networks, LLMs, or implementation details as selling points. Always translate tech into business outcome.
3. **No guru positioning**: Moroni is a peer operator, not a stage guru. No "I'll teach you", no "my exclusive method". Instead: "Here's the framework I built. It works. You'll build yours inside this program."
4. **No vague promises**: Every claim needs a number, timeframe, or concrete scenario. "Grow your revenue" is forbidden. "Add R$25K-R$80K per AI project to your monthly revenue" is the standard.
5. **No "ecosystem" or "jornada"**: These are humanization red flags. Use "programa", "estrutura", "parceria" instead.
6. **No desperation urgency**: No countdown timers, no "only 3 spots left!!!" Unless the scarcity is real and documented. Cohort size limits are real — state them once, calmly.
7. **No passive voice patterns**: "Sera ensinado" becomes "Voce vai construir". Active, first/second person.

### How This Differs from CEO Campaign

| Dimension | CEO Campaign | Agency Campaign |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Core promise | Clarity + Control over AI strategy | Revenue + Capability — a new service line |
| Primary emotion | Confidence (I lead AI, not react to it) | Urgency (I need this revenue stream NOW) |
| Decision frame | Strategic leadership | Operational ROI |
| Time horizon | Long-term positioning | First client in weeks, ROI in 90 days |
| Risk concern | "Am I making the right strategic bet?" | "Can I sell this? Can I deliver this? What if I fail?" |
| Moroni's role | Strategic advisor to the CEO | Peer operator who built the playbook |
| Proof that matters | Market data, methodology | Revenue numbers, peer success stories |
| CTA energy | "Apply when ready" (composed) | "Apply now — every month you wait costs you" (urgent but not desperate) |
