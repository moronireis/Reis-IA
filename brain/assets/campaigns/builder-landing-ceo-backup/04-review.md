### Pass 1: Brand Voice Audit

**[PASS] Consultivo premium tone.** The copy reads like a strategic briefing from a peer, not a sales pitch. Moroni is positioned as a fellow business strategist throughout. Lines like "Respeito o seu tempo. Se o fit não existir, eu falo isso direto na nossa conversa." perfectly embody the Porsche/Stripe confidence.

**[PASS] PT-BR natural.** The register is oral-formal Brazilian Portuguese. Anglicisms (ROI, framework, AI) are only those the audience already uses. Zero academicismo. Contractions like "pra" and "pro" feel natural without crossing into casual.

**[PASS] C-level appropriate.** Every sentence speaks to a business operator. No mention of APIs, code, LLMs, or technical implementation details. The language is revenue, P&L, board meetings, operational leverage — exactly the vocabulary of a R$5-50M CEO.

**[PASS] No prohibited brand elements.** No AI hype ("revolução", "disruptivo"), no superlativos without proof, no emojis, no vague promises. Every claim is anchored to a number or concrete scenario.

**[PASS] Reis IA identity test.** The Cemitério de Protótipos narrative, Revenue-First AI Framework, and Moroni's personal voice are distinctive. If you covered the name, the methodology language and the specific enemy narrative would still identify this as Reis IA. The chess/hourglass motifs are not present in copy (appropriate — those are visual, not textual).

**Minor note:** The word "nenhuma" on line 148 ("agência nenhuma") is grammatically informal but intentionally so — it fits the oral-formal register. This is a stylistic choice, not an error.

**Score: 9/10**

### Pass 2: Hormozi Framework Compliance

**[PASS] Value equation present.** The copy maximizes the numerator (R$50K-R$200K impact in 90 days, 74% ROI stat, guarantee of 3+ revenue opportunities) and minimizes the denominator (clarity on day one, CEO directs strategy without technical execution, 30-45 minute conversation as entry point).

**[PASS] All 4 persuasion angles covered:**
- More Good: Revenue mapping (3-5 opportunities), 65% lead qualification reduction, R$340K case study
- Less Bad: Cemitério de Protótipos narrative, decision paralysis eliminated, vendor dependency broken
- Status: "Ser o executivo que fez AI gerar receita de verdade — com dados, não com esperança" (Deliverable 6), board meetings narrative
- Social Risk: "Board, sócios ou investidores estão cobrando estratégia de AI" qualification bullet, Email 1 "a maioria nem admite"

**[PASS] Specificity over generality.** Data points are well-deployed: 42% abandonment, 29% can measure ROI, 74% achieve ROI, R$340K case study, 65% qualification time reduction, 22 hours/week recovered.

**[PASS] Cemitério de Protótipos enemy narrative.** Present in body copy (line 79), benefit block 2 title (line 136), Email 1 (line 209). The enemy is clearly the approach (tech-first), not the technology — exactly as the briefing requires.

**[PASS] WHO and WHEN layers explored.** Email sequence covers temporal progression (Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 5-7). Benefit blocks cover CEO self, board, and team perspectives. The qualification section addresses multiple stakeholder pressures (board, investors, competitors).

**[FLAG] Status and Social Risk angles could be deeper in the landing page body copy.** The email sequence does good work, but the landing page hero/body copy (Deliverables 1-3) lean heavily on More Good and Less Bad. Status and Social Risk appear mainly in Deliverables 6-7. Consider adding one status sentence to the hero body copy — even a brief aside about the board meeting scenario would strengthen the emotional range.

**[FLAG] WHO x WHEN matrix underutilized.** The briefing contains a rich 4x5 matrix for each angle. The copy uses maybe 20% of this material. The "1 year" timeframe is almost entirely absent from the landing page. This is a missed opportunity for a closing section or a "where you'll be in 12 months" block.

**Score: 8/10**

### Pass 3: Humanization Quality

**[FAIL] Prohibited word found: "transformou" (line 85).** The humanization rules explicitly prohibit "transformar" and its conjugations. The sentence reads: "O que separa o CEO que investiu em AI e se frustrou do CEO que transformou AI em linha de receita?" This must be replaced.

**[PASS] No prohibited structures.** No generic openings, no uniform paragraph lengths, no 5+ list items with identical grammatical openings. Deliverable 6 bullets were correctly varied (Sua empresa, Já investiu, Board, Você não é, Vê concorrentes, Está de saco cheio, Quer ser).

**[PASS] Rhythm variety.** The copy mixes short fragments ("Não por orçamento. Não por falta de empenho."), punchy one-liners ("Aí já era."), parenthetical asides, and longer explanatory sentences. Good cadence overall.

**[PASS] Human signals present.** Parenthetical asides work well: "(E olha que a maioria dos fornecedores de AI simplesmente ignora essa pergunta.)", "(E a maioria nem admite.)", "(E o investimento inicial em AI dos dois projetos anteriores? Virou aprendizado caro. Só isso.)". The "Funciona assim:" transitions, "Sem rodeio." close, and "Aí já era." fragment all read as natural human voice.

**[FLAG] "Transformação direta" label in Variation 3 heading (line 46).** While this is a section label rather than reader-facing copy, it still contains a derivative of the prohibited "transformar." Recommend changing to "Antes/depois direto" or "Contraste direto."

**[FLAG] Parenthetical asides cluster in a pattern.** Three of the four parenthetical asides follow the same structure: "(E [statement].)" — lines 73, 209, 287. While each individually is good, the repetition of the "(E..." opening across the full piece creates a detectable pattern. Vary at least one: consider dropping the "E" from one, or using a dash-based aside instead.

**[FLAG] Email 3 case study (lines 285-287) reads slightly over-crafted.** "R$18M de faturamento", "65%", "22 horas semanais", "R$340K" — four specific numbers in one paragraph is excellent for specificity but the paragraph structure (setup → metric → metric → metric → aside) is a recognizable AI pattern. Breaking this into two shorter paragraphs or adding a more casual transition between the metrics would improve naturalness.

**[FLAG] "Variação 5" headline (line 53) is long and structurally complex.** At 25+ words, it reads more like a subheadline than a headline variation. A headline this long will lose punch in A/B testing. Consider trimming.

**Score: 7/10** (the prohibited word "transformou" is the primary driver of the lower score)

### Issues Found

1. **[BLOCKING] Prohibited word "transformou" in Deliverable 3, Paragraph 3 (line 85).** "transformar" and all conjugations are on the humanization rules prohibited list. This word must be replaced before the copy can be approved.

2. **[MODERATE] Repetitive parenthetical aside pattern.** Three of four parenthetical asides open with "(E..." — creates a detectable AI fingerprint across the full piece.

3. **[MODERATE] "Transformação direta" in Variation 3 heading label (line 46).** Contains derivative of prohibited "transformar."

4. **[MINOR] Email 3 case study over-loaded with sequential metrics.** Four specific numbers in one paragraph without a natural pause reads slightly mechanical.

5. **[MINOR] Variation 5 headline too long.** At 25+ words it functions more as a subheadline and will underperform in A/B testing.

6. **[MINOR] Status/Social Risk angles underrepresented in hero section body copy.** Deliverables 1-3 lean almost entirely on More Good and Less Bad angles. The emotional range would benefit from one status reference in the hero section.

7. **[MINOR] Email 3 word count slightly over briefing spec.** The 150-200 word target is exceeded due to the case study and P.S.

### Recommended Changes

**Issue 1 — Prohibited word "transformou" (BLOCKING):**

Current (line 85):
> O que separa o CEO que investiu em AI e se frustrou do CEO que transformou AI em linha de receita?

Replace with:
> O que separa o CEO que investiu em AI e se frustrou do CEO que fez AI virar linha de receita?

The verb "fez...virar" is oral-formal, natural in Brazilian Portuguese, avoids the prohibited word, and maintains the punchy contrast.

**Issue 2 — Repetitive "(E..." asides:**

Change the aside on line 287 from:
> (E o investimento inicial em AI dos dois projetos anteriores? Virou aprendizado caro. Só isso.)

To:
> (O investimento dos dois projetos anteriores? Virou aprendizado caro. Só isso.)

Dropping the "E" from one of the three instances breaks the pattern.

**Issue 3 — "Transformação direta" label (line 46):**

Change from:
> **Variação 3 — Transformação direta (antes/depois):**

To:
> **Variação 3 — Contraste direto (antes/depois):**

**Issue 4 — Email 3 metrics clustering:**

Break the paragraph at line 285-287 into two:

> Um CEO de uma empresa de serviços profissionais — R$18M de faturamento — chegou pra essa mesma conversa depois de dois projetos de AI descontinuados.
>
> Em 90 dias, com o Revenue-First Framework, a equipe de vendas reduziu o tempo de qualificação de leads em 65%. Foram 22 horas semanais de capacity devolvidas pro fechamento. Receita atribuída à iniciativa no primeiro trimestre: R$340K.

This adds a natural pause between context and metrics.

**Issue 5 — Variation 5 too long:**

Current:
> Existe uma sequência que separa os 74% que têm ROI de AI dos 42% que desistem no meio do caminho. A maioria começa pelo passo errado.

Consider trimming to:
> 74% das empresas têm ROI de AI. 42% desistem. A diferença? A ordem em que fazem as coisas.

**Issue 6 — Status angle in hero section:**

Add one sentence at the end of Paragraph 1 (after line 73) or beginning of Paragraph 2:

> E quando o board pergunta "qual é a estratégia de AI?", a única resposta honesta é: "ainda não temos."

This weaves Social Risk naturally into the hero section without disrupting the validation → diagnosis flow.

### Strengths

1. **Cemitério de Protótipos narrative is excellent.** It names the enemy precisely, makes it concrete, and creates instant recognition for the burned buyer. This is a signature Reis IA concept and the copy uses it effectively without overplaying it.

2. **Data deployment is best-in-class.** 42%, 29%, 74%, R$340K, 65%, 22 hours — every stat earns its place and is anchored to a specific scenario. This is exactly what the Hormozi framework and the CMO briefing demanded.

3. **CTA variations are strong.** All three avoid "Learn More" or "Buy Now" generic patterns. "Mapear onde AI gera receita no meu negócio" is the standout — it frames the CTA as a business activity, not a sales interaction.

4. **Qualification/Disqualification blocks are excellent.** The "Para Quem É" section creates genuine "that's me" moments. The "Para Quem NÃO É" section is direct without being rude and the closing line ("Respeito o seu tempo...") perfectly embodies the peer-to-peer tone.

5. **Email sequence emotional arc works.** Day 1 validates, Day 3 reveals the mechanism, Day 5-7 presents the decision with social proof. The subject lines are punchy and avoid the over-polished structure common in AI-generated emails.

6. **The oral-formal register is well-calibrated.** "Bancou", "de saco cheio", "gaveta", "Aí já era" — these are words a Brazilian CEO would actually use in a board room hallway conversation. The humanization pass clearly improved the raw copy's register.

7. **Sells the conversation, not the program.** At no point does the copy slip into explaining program modules, tiers, or pricing. Every section funnels to one action: book the diagnostic conversation. This discipline is rare and correct for this ICP.
