# Gap Analysis — Builder Landing Page Copy (CEO/Founder ICP)

Last updated: 2026-03-17

> **Analyst**: direct-response-copywriter
> **Baseline Score**: 8.6/10
> **Target Score**: 9.5+/10
> **Objective**: Identify every weakness, energy drop, and missed opportunity section-by-section

---

## 1. HEADLINE PRINCIPAL + VARIAÇÕES

### Current:
"Você já colocou dinheiro em AI. O problema nunca foi a tecnologia — foi a ordem em que você fez as coisas."

### Gaps:

**Hormozi weakness — Dream Outcome absent.** The headline names the problem (wrong sequence) but never hints at the prize. A 9.5+ headline makes the reader feel the pain AND glimpse the upside in the same breath. Currently it's 100% diagnosis, 0% aspiration. The CEO reads this and thinks "ok, I messed up" but not "and here's what's possible."

**Energy drop — passive construction.** "A ordem em que você fez as coisas" is vague. Which things? The lack of specificity makes it feel like a consultant hedging. A CEO at R$5-50M needs to feel the punch of recognition, not a polite observation.

**PT-BR naturalness.** "A ordem em que você fez as coisas" has a translated-English feel. A Brazilian CEO would say "a sequência" or "o caminho" more naturally than "a ordem em que você fez as coisas." The construction "em que você fez" is grammatically correct but rhythmically flat.

**Variations analysis:**
- Variation 1 ("Seu projeto de AI rendeu demos bonitos. Receita? Zero.") — Strong punch but one-dimensional. No mechanism hint.
- Variation 2 — Good data but 42% stat is used 4+ times across the page. Overexposure risk.
- Variation 3 — "De CEO que bancou AI sem ver retorno — para CEO que faz AI pagar as contas" — strongest variation. Before/after + CEO identity language. Could be the primary.
- Variation 4 — "Por que empresas com metade do seu orçamento..." — comparison framing is good but "empresas" is generic. Not speaking to YOU.
- Variation 5 — Already trimmed per review. Still a subheadline disguised as a headline.

## 2. SUBHEADLINE — HERO SECTION

### Current:
"Só 29% dos executivos sabem dizer quanto AI devolveu de resultado..."

### Gaps:

**Too long.** 3 sentences in a subheadline. The reader's eye jumps from headline to subheadline looking for quick confirmation. This reads like a paragraph, not a subheadline. Should be 1-2 punchy sentences max.

**"Essa conversa vai mexer com a forma como você enxerga AI" — weak close.** "Mexer com a forma como você enxerga" is vague and passive. Sounds like a TED Talk promo, not a revenue conversation. A CEO doesn't want their worldview "mexida" — they want clarity on where the money is.

**Missing Revenue-First positioning.** The subheadline is the perfect place to plant the Revenue-First seed. Currently it only validates the problem without hinting at the mechanism.

---

## 3. BODY COPY — HERO SECTION

### Current: 6 paragraphs + framework intro

### Gaps:

**Energy drop at paragraph 3.** "Todo projeto de AI que morre compartilha o mesmo defeito de nascença." Good opening. But then: "A empresa começa pela ferramenta — escolhe a tecnologia, contrata quem constrói, monta o protótipo — e depois tenta enfiar aquilo no negócio." The dash-heavy construction slows the reading pace right when we need acceleration. Three dashes in one sentence = reader fatigue.

**"É como contratar a construtora antes de decidir onde você quer morar" — analogy mismatch.** A CEO building a R$5-50M company doesn't think in terms of residential construction. The analogy is accessible but it's consumer-grade, not executive-grade. Better: P&L language, board language, investment committee language.

**Missing Status/Social Risk in hero (flagged in review, not fully resolved).** The added board sentence ("E quando o board pergunta...") is good but isolated. It needs to be woven more naturally into the emotional arc, not dropped in as a patch.

**Paragraph 5 (Revenue-First intro) is too explanatory.** "O Revenue-First AI Framework existe pra inverter essa lógica. Na prática: mapeamos onde AI gera dinheiro no seu negócio específico — antes de abrir qualquer sprint, antes de qualquer linha de código." This tells instead of showing. "Existe pra inverter essa lógica" is a statement about the framework rather than a demonstration of what happens when you use it.

**Paragraph 6 (contrast paragraph) is good but predictable.** The "Não é X. Não é Y. É Z." structure appears multiple times across the page. By the third occurrence, the pattern becomes visible. Needs structural variety.

**Weak transition to CTA.** "Uma conversa de 30 a 45 minutos é o suficiente pra diagnosticar..." The word "suficiente" undersells. "O suficiente" implies bare minimum. A CEO reading this thinks "just barely enough?" — wrong frame. Should feel like a high-value 30-45 minutes, not a "just enough" interaction.

## 4. CTAs

### Current: 3 variations

### Gaps:

**CTA 1 ("Mapear onde AI gera receita no meu negócio") — strong but long.** At 9 words, it's pushing the limit for a button. Mobile truncation risk.

**CTA subtext lacks urgency.** "Conversa estratégica de 30 a 45 min com Moroni Reis — sem custo" — "sem custo" as the last thing the CEO reads creates a discount frame. Premium consultancies don't lead with "free." The subtext should emphasize exclusivity or scarcity, not absence of cost.

**No urgency mechanism anywhere.** Not a single scarcity or urgency element across any CTA. The review didn't flag this, but for 9.5+ you need authentic urgency: limited slots, calendar constraint, cohort timing — something real.

---

## 5. BLOCOS DE BENEFÍCIOS

### Gaps:

**Block 1 title ("Primeiro o mapa de receita. Implementação vem depois.") — instructional, not aspirational.** Tells the CEO what we do rather than what they get. Compare: "3 a 5 oportunidades de receita mapeadas antes de gastar R$1 em tecnologia."

**Block 2 title ("A sequência que fecha o Cemitério de Protótipos. De vez.") — good punch.** The "De vez." fragment works. Minor: "fecha" is ambiguous — closes as in shuts down? Or closes as in completes? "Enterra" would be sharper but might clash with the graveyard metaphor.

**Block 3 body — "agência nenhuma" grammar note aside, the benefit is diffuse.** "Você avalia, prioriza e mede iniciativas de AI do mesmo jeito que avalia qualquer investimento" — this is a feature (evaluation capability) dressed as a benefit. The real benefit: the CEO stops being dependent on vendors who don't understand their business. That's the emotional hook — dependency elimination.

**Missing Hourglass (time) narrative.** The briefing and implementation notes call for hourglass/time motif integration. Not a single time-recovery or time-as-asset paragraph exists in the benefits. The WHO x WHEN matrix shows "recover 15-30 hours/week" and "90-day first results" — none of this appears with emotional weight.

**No 12-month vision.** Review flagged: "The 1 year timeframe is almost entirely absent." Still true. A CEO making a R$20K-R$80K decision wants to see the 12-month picture: AI embedded as a P&L function, team operating with AI as standard procedure, competitive moat built.

---

## 6. PARA QUEM É

### Gaps:

**Bullet 5 ("Vê concorrentes implementando AI e sente a pressão de acertar desta vez") — too soft.** "Sente a pressão" is passive. CEOs don't "feel pressure" — they lose deals, lose talent, lose market share. Make it concrete.

**Missing financial specificity in qualification.** "Empresa fatura entre R$5M e R$50M" is stated but never made visceral. A R$5M CEO and a R$50M CEO have very different realities. Adding one scenario that bridges both would strengthen identification.

**7 bullets is at the edge of the humanization rule (5+ items with similar structure).** The varying openers help, but the list length itself creates a wall of text. Consider consolidating to 5-6 with more punch each.

---

## 7. PARA QUEM NÃO É

### Gaps:

**Generally strong.** The closing line ("Respeito o seu tempo...") is excellent. Minor issues:

**Bullet 3 revenue floor.** "Fatura abaixo de R$3M" — the ICP says R$5M minimum. Why disqualify at R$3M? This creates ambiguity for the R$3-5M range. Should match ICP: R$5M.

---

## 7.5. BLOCO DE GARANTIA

### Gaps:

**"Identifique Receita ou Continuamos Trabalhando" — title is clear but lacks visceral punch.** Compare: "3 Oportunidades de Receita ou Não Paramos Até Encontrar." Specificity in the guarantee headline itself increases belief.

**Body too short.** Two sentences. A guarantee this strong deserves 3-4 sentences that reinforce it. Add: what "concretas" means, what "ROI projetado" looks like in practice, and the implied confidence behind it.

---

## 7.6. BLOCO DE CREDIBILIDADE

### Gaps:

**Generic proof.** "Dezenas de projetos" is vague. The Hormozi framework demands specificity: how many? What industries? What results? "Dezenas" is the word of someone who doesn't have real numbers.

**Missing personal narrative.** Moroni's story is absent. Why does he care? What did HE see that made him build this framework? A 2-3 sentence origin story dramatically increases trust with C-level buyers who are evaluating the person, not the company.

**No social proof stack.** Zero testimonials, zero logos, zero named results. The case study in Email 3 is a placeholder. On the landing page itself, there is NO proof element beyond Moroni's bio. For a CEO investing R$20K-R$80K, this is a critical gap.

## 8. EMAIL SEQUENCE

### Gaps:

**Email 1 — solid.** Only gap: "aposto que um pensamento ficou" is slightly presumptuous for a first touch. Could soften to a question.

**Email 2 — strongest of the three.** The 1-2-3 framework reveal works. P.S. is missing (briefing says 2 of 3 — currently satisfied, but Email 2 would benefit from one).

**Email 3 — placeholder case study is a major credibility gap.** The entire email's persuasive weight rests on a fabricated scenario. Until real data exists, this email underperforms significantly. The "two paths" framework is strong, but without real proof, it's a logical argument without emotional anchor.

## CROSS-CUTTING GAPS

### 1. Revenue-First Positioning Not Sharp Enough
The framework is mentioned but never made visceral. "Começamos pela receita" is stated multiple times but never demonstrated with a concrete walkthrough. Show the CEO what the first 30 minutes look like. What questions are asked. What the output looks like.

### 2. "Não é X. Não é Y. É Z." Pattern Overuse
This rhetorical structure appears in: headline, body paragraph 6, benefit block 1 body, benefit block 3 body. By the fourth occurrence, it reads as a template, not a voice.

### 3. 42% Stat Fatigue
The 42% abandonment stat appears in: headline variation 2, subheadline area, body copy paragraph 1, benefit block 2, Email 1, Email 2. Six appearances. By the third, the reader is numb to it. Reserve for 2-3 high-impact placements.

### 4. Hourglass (Time) Narrative Missing
The time dimension — CEO time as the scarcest resource, time-to-revenue, time recovered for the team — is dramatically underutilized. This should be a thematic thread, not an afterthought. The briefing notes: "The CEO is buying TIME and CLARITY."

### 5. Premium Tone Drops
- "sem custo" in CTA subtext (discount frame)
- "suficiente" in CTA transition (bare minimum frame)
- "Funciona assim:" used twice (pattern)
- Benefit block 3 "agência nenhuma" (slightly too casual for this section's gravitas)

### 6. Translated-English Phrasing
- "a ordem em que você fez as coisas" (the order in which you did things)
- "essa conversa vai mexer com a forma como você enxerga AI" (this conversation will mess with the way you see AI)
- "alcançam ROI mensurável" (achieve measurable ROI) — "alcançam" in this context reads like a translation of "achieve"

### 7. Missing Objection Handling
The top 7 objections from the briefing are barely addressed on the landing page:
- "We tried before and it didn't work" — addressed (Cemitério de Protótipos)
- "How is this different?" — partially (framework mentioned, not demonstrated)
- "Can you guarantee ROI?" — addressed (guarantee block)
- "How long before results?" — NOT addressed (no timeline on landing page)
- "Will it work with our systems?" — NOT addressed
- "I don't have time for 12 weeks" — NOT addressed
- "Why mentorship, not done-for-you?" — NOT addressed

Three of seven objections are completely unaddressed. For 9.5+, at least 5-6 need explicit or implicit answers.
