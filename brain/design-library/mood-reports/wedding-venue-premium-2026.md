# Mood Report — Wedding Venue Premium 2026

> **Project**: Castelo dos Lagos redesign
> **Branded house**: Shared type DNA with Buffet Di Matoso; palette variant = deep forest green
> **Last updated**: 2026-04-15
> **Curator**: visual-research-scout
> **Ceiling reference (approved)**: https://castelo-estrategia.vercel.app/ — Cormorant Garamond + Inter, charcoal #1A1A1A + gold #C9A96E + cream #FAFAF7, editorial serif numerals, fade-up scroll reveals.
> **Mission**: push UP from this ceiling. Less gold, more forest. Less wedding, more estate. Less SaaS, more magazine.

---

## Intent

Castelo dos Lagos is a multi-generational family estate for weddings and large celebrations, not a "wedding venue". The digital experience should read like an Aman property or a Belmond estate — confident, quiet, architectural, photographed like a Cereal magazine spread. Zero rose/blush. Zero script fonts. Zero glitter. Zero Pinterest-bride clichés. The palette pivots from the ceiling reference's charcoal+gold toward **deep forest green + cream + bronze + warm shadow**, keeping the editorial serif spine.

The references below are ORGANIZED BY COVERAGE AREA, not ranked. Each entry is a real, verifiable URL — no fabricated refs.

## 1. Cinematic Hero Photography — Scale, Golden Hour, Architecture

### 1.1 Passalacqua Lake Como
- **URL**: https://www.passalacqua.it/en/
- **Visual**: 18th-century villa shot at golden hour from lake side, full-bleed hero, tiny serif wordmark, no navigation clutter. Photography composed as if for a coffee-table book.
- **Reusable**: hero as a SINGLE architectural photograph that occupies 100vh, wordmark micro-sized top-left, one CTA max. The architecture itself carries the headline — copy is secondary.
- **Do NOT reuse**: saturated Italian-pastel color grading (too tourist). Castelo needs deeper, cooler greens.
- **Why for Castelo**: the property is the product. A single hero that sells the estate in one frame is more persuasive than any carousel.

### 1.2 Hotel Il Pellicano (Pellicano Hotels)
- **URL**: https://www.pellicanohotels.com/en/hotels/hotel-il-pellicano/
- **Visual**: vintage-tinged photography, sun-bleached editorial, muted coral and olive tones, serif wordmark, grid of lifestyle + architecture.
- **Reusable**: editorial mix of wide architectural shots and intimate detail shots (a hand on a wineglass, an empty chair in a garden). This dual grammar — monumental + intimate — is what separates venue sites from hotel sites.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the beach/striped-umbrella visual codes (wrong context for inland castle).
- **Why for Castelo**: demonstrates how to photograph a property so every frame feels like a film still. The wedding is implied, never depicted.

### 1.3 Belmond — portfolio hub
- **URL**: https://www.belmond.com/
- **Visual**: full-bleed hero video/photo loops of iconic properties; navigation as editorial menu with serif; strong editorial rhythm between sections.
- **Reusable**: hero video loop at 0.5x slowed, no ken-burns zoom. Subtle, atmospheric, almost still. Cut points tied to scroll, not time.
- **Do NOT reuse**: global-brand corporate voice ("Luxury journeys worldwide"). Castelo must speak local, specific, familial.
- **Why for Castelo**: proves a premium venue can lead with motion that feels restrained, not flashy.

### 1.4 Castello di Vicarello
- **URL**: https://www.castellodivicarello.com/
- **Visual**: Tuscan stone estate photographed in warm ambient light; serif headlines; muted earth palette (terracotta, olive, cream, warm grey).
- **Reusable**: the idea that a castle can be photographed WITHOUT medieval clichés — no armor, no flags, no banquet tables. Just architecture, light, landscape.
- **Do NOT reuse**: terracotta orange — doesn't align with forest green direction.
- **Why for Castelo**: closest direct peer (castle estate for weddings). Study the photographic restraint.

### 1.5 Borgo Santo Pietro
- **URL**: https://borgosantopietro.com/
- **Visual**: 13th century villa presented as a lived-in estate — gardens, interiors, food, landscapes — each photo in editorial composition with negative space.
- **Reusable**: the "editorial inventory" approach — the website is an unhurried tour, not a sales funnel.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the botanical illustration accents (too Etsy for Castelo).
- **Why for Castelo**: a family venue should feel inhabited, not staged.

### 1.6 COMO Castello Del Nero
- **URL**: https://www.comohotels.com/italy/como-castello-del-nero
- **Visual**: 12th century castle in Chianti; minimal dark navigation, large serif hero with restrained motion.
- **Reusable**: the way a single line of serif type becomes the entire hero (no subheadline, no CTA stack).
- **Do NOT reuse**: global-chain navigation density.
- **Why for Castelo**: direct peer in the "castle as venue" category.

## 2. Editorial Serif Typography — Display Serif + Structural Sans

### 2.1 Kinfolk (custom Schick Toikka serif + sans pair)
- **URL**: https://www.kinfolk.com/
- **Visual**: custom Kinfolk Sans + Serif share vertical metrics and proportions, so they mix without friction. Long-form editorial stories with generous leading.
- **Reusable**: the pairing philosophy — serif for display and pull-quotes, sans for body and UI. Both at the same x-height so they visually rhyme.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the Kinfolk "slow living" tone-of-voice (too earnest for Castelo).
- **Why for Castelo**: proves Cormorant (display) + Inter (structural) can behave as one type system if you harmonize vertical metrics. This is exactly the branded-house discipline needed across Castelo + Buffet Di Matoso.

### 2.2 Cereal Magazine
- **URL**: https://www.readcereal.com/
- **Visual**: austere serif wordmark, grid-based section markers, tiny caption-style sans metadata under images. Travel photography in muted palette.
- **Reusable**: section numbering with serif numerals (01, 02, 03) as structural anchors — already in the ceiling reference, push further. Captions in small sans caps beneath photos.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the near-monochrome rigor (Castelo has more warmth).
- **Why for Castelo**: Cereal's editorial scaffolding is the blueprint for how to number, caption, and label architectural photography without decoration.

### 2.3 Aman Resorts
- **URL**: https://www.aman.com/
- **Visual**: art-directed page-by-page; editorial concept called "Meditations"; subtle, slow interactions; type pairs a custom serif with a thin sans.
- **Reusable**: the principle that motion should "allow content to shine through" — interactions are felt more than seen. One fade per scroll event, nothing competing.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the custom-typeface investment isn't on the table; stick with Cormorant + Inter.
- **Why for Castelo**: Aman is the global ceiling for restraint. Our aesthetic goal is "if Aman built a Brazilian estate site".

### 2.4 Castello di Ristonchi
- **URL**: https://www.ristonchi.com/
- **Visual**: 1,000-year Tuscan castle — serif heads, warm cream background, oversized landscape photography, quiet navigation.
- **Reusable**: hero headline as a single serif sentence (no subtitle). The type size is so large it becomes compositional.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the ivory background — Castelo goes darker (forest + cream inversion for most sections).
- **Why for Castelo**: validates the single-sentence serif hero pattern for a real castle venue.

### 2.5 Gargonza Castle
- **URL**: https://gargonza.it/en/
- **Visual**: medieval Tuscan castle, full-width photography, elegant serif, understated navigation.
- **Reusable**: the use of section TITLES in serif but section BODIES in humanist sans — a clear hierarchy without visual noise.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the somewhat dated carousel interactions.
- **Why for Castelo**: proves the editorial serif/sans split scales to venue content (rooms, experiences, history, contact).

### 2.6 Babington House (Soho House)
- **URL**: https://www.sohohouse.com/houses/babington-house
- **Visual**: Georgian country house photographed in low warm light, serif + grotesk sans pair, moody interiors, zero wedding marketing language.
- **Reusable**: the refusal to call itself a "venue" or "wedding destination" — it describes itself as a place, and weddings are a consequence. Type and photography do the selling.
- **Do NOT reuse**: members-club language (exclusive, private, etc.).
- **Why for Castelo**: positioning lesson — Castelo is an estate that hosts celebrations, not a wedding venue.

## 3. Dark Natural Palettes — Forest, Charcoal, Cream, Earth

### 3.1 Castello Il Palagio (Chianti)
- **URL**: https://castelloilpalagio.eu/
- **Visual**: medieval Chianti castle wedding venue — warm stone, deep garden greens, ochre sunsets, ivory interiors.
- **Reusable**: the chromatic vocabulary of the estate itself becomes the palette. Forest green + stone cream + warm shadow + bronze highlight. Pull the palette from an actual photo of the property, not a brand system deck.
- **Do NOT reuse**: italic script accents in the identity.
- **Why for Castelo**: closest palette sibling. Prove the forest green thesis with real photographic evidence from a peer castle.

### 3.2 Castello di Segalari
- **URL**: https://www.castellosegalari.com/
- **Visual**: hilltop castle, dense vineyards, greens and warm stone; editorial photography dominates.
- **Reusable**: the deep green of the vineyard canopy as the dominant surface color — not painted green, PHOTOGRAPHED green. Castelo should do the same with its lakes/gardens.
- **Do NOT reuse**: rustic wood texture overlays.
- **Why for Castelo**: palette proof-of-concept for "forest green as atmosphere, not as brand swatch".

### 3.3 The Drake Hotel (Awwwards SOTD, Sep 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.thedrakehotel.ca/
- **Visual**: boutique hotel Toronto/Prince Edward County; dark moody palette, editorial layout, strong serif identity.
- **Reusable**: the dark-mode-first approach for a hospitality brand. Deep charcoal + cream + single accent. Proves dark-mode works for venues (most venue sites are light-mode default).
- **Do NOT reuse**: the Drake's quirky cultural voice — too casual for Castelo.
- **Why for Castelo**: ceiling reference already uses charcoal-dominant. Drake is the award-recognized proof that this works at scale.

### 3.4 Kimpton Vividora Barcelona (Awwwards, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.kimptonvividora.com/
- **Visual**: boutique hotel Barcelona; warm earth palette with terracotta, cream, forest accents; editorial storytelling.
- **Reusable**: the layered earth palette (not flat surfaces — gradients of warm shadow). Castelo can use a subtle vertical gradient from near-black forest at the top of each section to warm cream at the bottom.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the terracotta heat (belongs to Buffet Di Matoso variant, maybe — confirm with brand strategist).
- **Why for Castelo**: branded-house palette split — if Di Matoso can own the warm terracotta and Castelo owns the forest green, the two sites read as siblings.

### 3.5 Kimpton Los Monteros Marbella (Awwwards, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.kimptonlosmonterosmarbella.com/
- **Visual**: Mediterranean resort, cream + deep olive + muted coral, editorial hero reveals.
- **Reusable**: the cream-as-ground + deep-accent-as-figure approach. Most surfaces are warm cream; forest green and bronze only appear where they carry meaning.
- **Do NOT reuse**: Mediterranean beach iconography.
- **Why for Castelo**: balances the "dark mode default" inclination with the reality that brides and families browsing at noon on mobile need warm cream surfaces for long-form content.

## 4. Editorial Grid — NOT SaaS Grid

### 4.1 ISSIMO (Pellicano digital extension)
- **URL**: https://issimoissimo.com/
- **Visual**: editorial magazine built by a hotel group; Italian lifestyle journalism, serif masthead, asymmetric grid, cover stories.
- **Reusable**: the asymmetric editorial grid — columns of different widths, captions floating, photos breaking the column, pull-quotes in oversized serif. This is the OPPOSITE of the SaaS 12-column rigidity.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the e-commerce product-page patterns (ISSIMO also sells goods — ignore that surface).
- **Why for Castelo**: proves a hospitality brand can run a proper editorial grid on the web. Castelo's "History", "Estate", "Celebrations" sections should use this grammar.

### 4.2 Cereal Magazine — city guides
- **URL**: https://www.readcereal.com/city-guides
- **Visual**: long-form travel guides with full-bleed photography alternating with narrow serif text columns. White space as structural element.
- **Reusable**: the column-width rhythm (wide photo / narrow text / wide photo / narrow text) as a scroll pattern for Castelo's "Experience the estate" page.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the column layouts at the narrowest mobile breakpoints tend to be too rigid — needs adaptation.
- **Why for Castelo**: city guide = estate tour. Same content shape.

### 4.3 Kinfolk features
- **URL**: https://www.kinfolk.com/features/
- **Visual**: editorial features index; grid of story cards with serif headlines, muted imagery, generous whitespace between cards.
- **Reusable**: the "story card" as a reusable unit for the "Celebrations we've hosted" gallery. Each event becomes a mini editorial feature, not a testimonial block.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the dense magazine tagging.
- **Why for Castelo**: reframes the inevitable "past weddings gallery" as editorial portfolio instead of social-proof wall.

## 5. Location Photography Staging

### 5.1 Le Sirenuse Positano
- **URL**: https://sirenuse.it/en/
- **Visual**: fifth design iteration; emotional, immersive photography; vertical reveals; large type over color-washed photos.
- **Reusable**: the photograph-first hero where type sits OVER the photo but uses a solid color bar behind the crucial serif headline so it remains legible without scrims or overlays.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the iconic red-and-white beach umbrellas (location-specific).
- **Why for Castelo**: solves the "legibility on photographic hero" problem without resorting to dark gradient scrims (which feel cheap).

### 5.2 Aman Villas gallery
- **URL**: https://www.aman.com/villas
- **Visual**: each villa photographed in consistent lighting and color grade, across wildly different locations, so the brand unifies the portfolio.
- **Reusable**: the color-grade discipline — Castelo should commission a photographer to produce ONE cohesive set shot in similar light (golden hour or blue hour), not mix vendor photography.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the global-spa aesthetic.
- **Why for Castelo**: the single biggest lever for premium perception is photographic consistency. Most venue sites lose premium points from mixing 6 photographers' styles.

### 5.3 Castelo de Itaipava / Castelo Saint Andrews (Brazilian reference, search as comparison)
- **URL**: https://www.castelosaintandrews.com.br/
- **Visual**: Brazilian high-end castle venue; useful as a BASELINE to beat — study what NOT to do in the domestic market.
- **Reusable**: understanding the domestic ceiling so we can push past it. Most BR venue sites over-decorate, over-gallery, and under-edit.
- **Do NOT reuse**: most of it. Specifically avoid the gallery grids, the "depoimentos" pattern, the multi-column-footer.
- **Why for Castelo**: a local negative example is as valuable as a global positive one.

## 6. Restrained Editorial Motion — Fade, Parallax, Slow

### 6.1 Aman — page transitions
- **URL**: https://www.aman.com/hotels/amangiri
- **Visual**: section-to-section fade with a slight vertical drift; no JS snap-scroll; Lenis-style smoothness.
- **Reusable**: Lenis smooth scroll + GSAP ScrollTrigger fade-up with translateY(24px→0) and opacity(0→1), duration 0.9s, ease "power2.out". Single motion language across the entire site.
- **Do NOT reuse**: any parallax above 40px displacement (starts to feel theme-park).
- **Why for Castelo**: the ceiling reference uses fade-up already — this is the scaled, art-directed version.

### 6.2 Passalacqua — hero cinemagraph
- **URL**: https://www.passalacqua.it/en/
- **Visual**: hero contains a near-still video loop — lake water moving, tree leaves shifting, everything else frozen. Cinemagraph territory.
- **Reusable**: cinemagraph technique for the Castelo hero — still photograph of the estate with ONE moving element (lake surface, leaves, candle flame, drapery). Much more premium than a full-motion video.
- **Do NOT reuse**: auto-play with audio (never).
- **Why for Castelo**: distinguishes Castelo from every competitor using stock drone footage.

### 6.3 Kimpton Vividora — reveals
- **URL**: https://www.kimptonvividora.com/
- **Visual**: soft mask reveals on images as they enter viewport; serif headlines slide in after images land.
- **Reusable**: the ORDER of reveal — image first, then type 200ms later. This sequencing feels directed instead of automated.
- **Do NOT reuse**: the easing curves that overshoot (back.out) — too playful for Castelo.
- **Why for Castelo**: motion choreography as editorial art direction.

## Anti-patterns — Explicit Prohibitions for Castelo dos Lagos

These appear across the wedding-venue category. They must not enter the Castelo build.

1. **Rose/blush/peach color accents** — the entire "wedding palette" canon is off-limits.
2. **Script cursive invitation fonts** (Allura, Great Vibes, Pinyon Script, any handwritten "elegant" font). Cormorant is as decorative as we get.
3. **Glitter, sparkle, shimmer, any animated sparkle/particle effect.**
4. **Heart icons, ring icons, floral dividers, ornamental flourishes.**
5. **"Your dream wedding" / "the day of your dreams" / "forever starts here" copy language.**
6. **Testimonial carousels** with quote marks and italic text ("Magical! — Maria & João"). Replace with editorial case studies.
7. **SaaS pricing tiers** ("Basic / Premium / Luxury packages"). CTAs route to "Agendar visita" / "Aplicar para evento".
8. **Gold. Specifically, tacky gold.** The ceiling reference gold (#C9A96E) is the MAXIMUM gold allowed — Castelo should pull further toward bronze/warm shadow, and the primary accent should be forest green anyway.
9. **Stock drone footage** of generic European castles in 4K montage.
10. **"Wedding packages" pricing pages.** Replace with "Experiences" or "Celebrations" as editorial content.
11. **Hero video with auto-playing orchestral music.**
12. **Multi-photographer galleries** with inconsistent color grades.
13. **Emoji, icons-as-decoration, or any Material/Feather icon set for navigation.**

## Direção recomendada pro Castelo dos Lagos

Opinionated distillation for art-director Phase 2. Pull these eight directions directly from this mood report:

1. **Palette**: deep forest green (#1E2E24 → #2F4A3A range) as primary surface, cream (#F4EFE6) as secondary surface, warm bronze (#8A6A3E, not gold) as accent, near-black (#0E0E0C) for type on cream. The palette is PHOTOGRAPHIC first — pull colors from actual hero photography of the estate, not from a brand swatch. Reference: Castello Il Palagio, Castello di Segalari, Kimpton Los Monteros.

2. **Type system**: Cormorant Garamond (display, weights 300-500) + Inter (structural, weights 400-500). Harmonize vertical metrics so they rhyme visually (Kinfolk discipline). Hero headlines are SINGLE serif sentences, no subheadline stack. Section titles serif, body humanist sans. Numerals use Roman (I. II. III.) for chapter markers — more architectural than the ceiling reference's 01/02/03.

3. **Hero grammar**: ONE cinemagraph or single photograph occupying 100vh. Estate architecture at golden hour OR blue hour. Tiny serif wordmark top-left. Single CTA ("Agendar visita") top-right. Headline in oversized Cormorant, one sentence, no subtitle. Reference: Passalacqua, Castello di Vicarello, COMO Castello Del Nero.

4. **Photography direction**: commission ONE photographer to produce the entire estate shoot in consistent golden hour grade. Mix wide architectural frames with intimate details (a hand on stone, candlelight, lake surface, draped fabric). Implied celebration, never depicted celebration. No guests in frame. Reference: Aman Villas, Il Pellicano editorial.

5. **Grid**: asymmetric editorial, NOT SaaS 12-column. Columns of varying width, captions floating, pull-quotes in oversized serif breaking the column. Wide photo / narrow text / wide photo rhythm for long scroll. Reference: ISSIMO, Cereal city guides, Kinfolk features.

6. **Motion language**: Lenis smooth scroll + GSAP ScrollTrigger. Single fade-up pattern: translateY(24px→0) + opacity(0→1), 0.9s, power2.out. Image reveals precede type by 200ms. Zero parallax above 40px. Cinemagraph hero, no auto-play video. Reference: Aman, Passalacqua, Kimpton Vividora.

7. **Structure as estate tour**: homepage is a 7-chapter scroll story with Roman numeral chapter markers: I. Arrival · II. Os Lagos · III. Arquitetura · IV. Cerimônia · V. Salão · VI. Suítes · VII. Convite. Each chapter is one photograph + one serif sentence + one paragraph of humanist sans. No "Features / Benefits / Testimonials / CTA" SaaS layout. Reference: Aman Meditations, ISSIMO long-form, Pellicano Our Story.

8. **Positioning rewrite**: Castelo dos Lagos is an ESTATE, not a venue. The copy never says "wedding venue". It says "a propriedade", "a casa", "o castelo". Weddings are a consequence of the estate's existence, not its product. The family (stewardship language) is front and center. Reference: Babington House, Borgo Santo Pietro — neither calls itself a wedding venue, both host them.
