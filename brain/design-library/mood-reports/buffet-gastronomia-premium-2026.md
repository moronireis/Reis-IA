## Context

Buffet Di Matoso is a family-run premium Brazilian catering/buffet for weddings and high-end events. It sits in a branded house with Castelo dos Lagos — shared typography base (Cormorant Garamond display + Inter structural), variant palette. The Di Matoso variant lands on **deep gold over charcoal** — editorial, not ostentatious. Think Kinfolk-meets-Michelin-guide, not country-club-wedding-brochure.

The enemy aesthetic we must avoid at all costs:
- Saturated credit-card gold (#FFD700-range)
- Script "invitation" cursive fonts
- Flat-lay food on pure white (iFood / Uber Eats visual language)
- Stock images of champagne glasses clinking
- Rose-gold gradient overlays
- Ornate baroque flourishes, filigree borders, damask patterns

## 1. Gastronomic Hero — Food Photography as Art

### 1.1 Noma — https://noma.dk/
**Visual**: Full-bleed seasonal photography, no hero copy fighting the image. Restrained UI. Natural light, Nordic austerity. The plate is the headline.
**Reuse**: Hero as a single, oversized, perfectly lit plate shot — zero chrome, zero chips/buttons over it. Menu and booking tucked into a corner. Photography is 95% of the storytelling.
**Don't reuse**: The stark Nordic cream-white palette — Di Matoso needs warmth. Keep the restraint, change the temperature.
**Why**: Proves that a single great image replaces three paragraphs of copy. Gives Di Matoso permission to be quieter than competitors.

### 1.2 Eleven Madison Park — https://www.elevenmadisonpark.com/
**Visual**: Editorial vertical layout, black-and-white heritage photography, serif display with generous tracking, almost no chrome. Feels like a museum card, not a restaurant site.
**Reuse**: Monochrome + single gold hairline accent. Large-format serif in uppercase with wide letter-spacing for headings (not for body). The "museum label" treatment of dishes.
**Don't reuse**: The full B&W commitment — Di Matoso needs the deep gold to carry warmth.
**Why**: Establishes the editorial ceiling for fine dining on the web. Di Matoso should feel 70% EMP restraint + 30% Brazilian warmth.

### 1.3 Geranium — https://www.geranium.dk/
**Visual**: Cinematic close-ups of plates against dark wood and stone. Scroll-driven reveal of courses. Heavy reliance on environment photography (kitchen, hands, ingredients in situ).
**Reuse**: Dark earthy backgrounds (charcoal with deep warm tint, not flat black), scroll-triggered plate reveals, environment B-roll between plates.
**Don't reuse**: Their full parallax video autoplay — too heavy and too cool-toned.
**Why**: Exact temperature model for "editorial darkness" — not black, but warm graphite. This is where Di Matoso should land visually.

### 1.4 Mirazur (Mauro Colagreco) — https://www.mirazur.fr/
**Visual**: Full-screen garden/ocean hero, serif display over photography, hand-written annotation accents. Romantic without being saccharine.
**Reuse**: The "landscape as context for the food" move — Di Matoso can use Brazilian light, terroir shots, hand gestures. Hero can be a hand placing a garnish, not the plate itself.
**Don't reuse**: The turquoise/teal Mediterranean tones.
**Why**: Shows how to make a website feel like a place, not a menu. Di Matoso should feel rooted — family, garden, fire.

### 1.5 Pujol — https://pujol.com.mx/
**Visual**: Terracotta + charcoal + cream editorial palette. Large serif display, Spanish-language confidence. Plates shot against raw clay and wood surfaces.
**Reuse**: Warm palette discipline — ONE warm accent (Di Matoso: deep gold, Pujol: terracotta) + charcoal + cream. Never 3 warm accents competing.
**Don't reuse**: Terracotta itself (too close to what we're banning).
**Why**: Demonstrates that a warm non-blue palette CAN read editorial if the discipline is Swiss.

## 2. Typography — Display Serif + Editorial Italic + Structural Sans

### 2.1 Central Restaurante — https://centralrestaurante.com.pe/en/
**Visual**: Large serif headings, italic subheads used as editorial captions (not decoration), structural sans for nav and metadata. The italic carries the emotional weight; the sans carries the information.
**Reuse**: **Italic as a voice, not an accent** — use italic Cormorant Garamond for chef quotes, dish poetry, stories. Use Inter for ingredients list, prices, times.
**Don't reuse**: Their specific serif choice (it's a custom face).
**Why**: This is the unlock — italic in service of storytelling, not ornament. Di Matoso's typographic signature should be this italic/sans dialogue.

### 2.2 Atomix — https://www.atomixnyc.com/
**Visual**: Extreme whitespace, single-page editorial flow, serif display in restrained weight, numerical course markers (I / II / III / IV) set as editorial sidebars.
**Reuse**: **Roman-numeral course markers** as a navigational/structural motif. Massive whitespace between sections (200px+ desktop). One idea per screen.
**Don't reuse**: The full single-page commitment — Di Matoso needs service pages for events/weddings/corporate.
**Why**: Gives us a typographic structural device (numerals as anchors) that feels editorial without cliché.

### 2.3 A Casa do Porco — https://acasadoporco.com.br/
**Visual**: Bold, confident, Brazilian. Uses red accent + charcoal + serif display with personality. Casa do Porco is proof a Brazilian restaurant site can read international-editorial.
**Reuse**: The confidence and the **Portuguese-first copy rhythm** — don't dilute with English-first patterns. Di Matoso's voice should be PT-BR editorial, not translated.
**Don't reuse**: Red accent, the rustic wood textures.
**Why**: Local benchmark — proves Brazilian premium doesn't mean stock "elegante" aesthetic. Di Matoso should steal the confidence, not the palette.

### 2.4 Oteque — https://www.oteque.com/
**Visual**: Minimal, almost brutalist restraint. Serif + sans binary. Nearly monochrome. Lets the Michelin star credibility do the talking.
**Reuse**: Permission to be almost empty. If the food and the family story are strong, the site can be 4 screens total.
**Don't reuse**: The near-total coolness — feels almost clinical.
**Why**: Upper-bound on restraint. Di Matoso should be more emotional than Oteque but inherit the "less is more" discipline.

## 3. Warm Editorial Palettes — Deep Gold, Cream, Charcoal, Burgundy, Olive

### 3.1 Le Meurice Alain Ducasse — https://www.alainducasse-meurice.com/en
**Visual**: Deep bordeaux + cream + charcoal + restrained gold hairlines. Gold is used as a LINE, not a FILL. Thin gold rules separate sections, never gold blocks.
**Reuse**: **Gold as hairline / rule / underline only** — never as a button fill, never as a background. Gold used in 1-2px thickness, max 8% screen coverage.
**Don't reuse**: The Parisian palace formality — too stiff for family Brazilian.
**Why**: This is THE rule that keeps gold from going tacky. Discipline: gold lives in strokes, not fills.

### 3.2 The French Laundry / Thomas Keller Restaurant Group — https://www.thomaskeller.com/tfl/
**Visual**: Blue-navy + cream + serif. Zero gold, actually. But the editorial pacing, the hero treatment, the menu-as-manuscript — transferable wholesale.
**Reuse**: **Menu as manuscript** layout — courses presented as a printed program, not a list. Centered text column, generous leading, italic flourishes for course names.
**Don't reuse**: The navy palette — just re-skin this layout in charcoal+gold.
**Why**: The "printed wedding program as website" metaphor — perfect for Di Matoso's wedding-buffet positioning.

### 3.3 Blue by Alain Ducasse — https://www.blue-alainducasse.com/
**Visual**: Deep blue-black with single warm accent. Editorial photography, centered compositions.
**Reuse**: The single-accent discipline on a dark field. Swap blue-black for charcoal-warm, swap their accent for Di Matoso's deep gold.
**Don't reuse**: The blue itself.
**Why**: Structural template — one dark field, one warm accent, editorial serif, done.

### 3.4 Restaurant GEM (Awwwards Nominee) — https://www.awwwards.com/sites/restaurant-gem
**Visual**: Warm refined palette, subtle motion, editorial pacing, booking flow integrated without breaking the aesthetic.
**Reuse**: Booking flow treated as editorial content, not a SaaS form. Date picker styled like a printed calendar. CTA as a serif line, not a pill button.
**Don't reuse**: N/A — reuse liberally.
**Why**: Proves the booking/reservation flow can stay editorial. Di Matoso's "solicitar orçamento" must follow this treatment, not a Calendly pill.

## 4. Editorial Menu Storytelling (Not Delivery Menu)

### 4.1 Noma seasonal menu pages — https://noma.dk/ (see Game Season, Vegetable Season, Ocean Season sub-pages)
**Visual**: Each season is a full editorial spread. Essay-length copy about the ingredient philosophy. Dishes as a list with Roman numerals, no prices, no "Add to cart."
**Reuse**: **Seasonal/thematic menus as essays.** Di Matoso can present wedding menus as "Menu Outono," "Menu Colheita," each one a narrative, not a price list.
**Don't reuse**: The zero-price commitment — Di Matoso still needs to signal premium via "orçamento sob consulta."
**Why**: Repositions menu from "ordering interface" to "editorial story." Massive differentiator from competing Brazilian buffet sites.

### 4.2 Mirazur menu — https://www.mirazur.fr/le-restaurant.php
**Visual**: Menu items as handwritten-feel typography over photographs. Each course has its own small vignette.
**Reuse**: Per-course visual vignette — small square photo + 2-line italic poetic description + ingredient list in sans.
**Don't reuse**: Handwriting fonts (banned).
**Why**: The "one vignette per course" pattern — structurally elegant, infinitely extensible.

### 4.3 Atomix course cards — https://www.atomixnyc.com/
**Visual**: Atomix famously presents each course on a small printed card in the restaurant. The website echoes this — each dish gets a card-sized presentation block.
**Reuse**: **"Printed card" motif** — each dish is a small card with serif dish name, italic subhead, tiny numbered marker, no buttons. Web version of the physical card.
**Don't reuse**: N/A.
**Why**: Translates a physical ritual into a web motif. Di Matoso can do "carta do buffet" — each canape, each course, each dessert gets a card.

## 5. Food Macro Photography — Textures, Hands, Ingredients

### 5.1 Pujol ingredient photography — https://pujol.com.mx/acerca-de/
**Visual**: Macro shots of corn, mole jars, hands cracking peppers. Warm tungsten-esque lighting, shallow DOF, zero props.
**Reuse**: **Hands in photography** — hands plating, hands pouring, hands cutting. Never the chef's face in closeup as a hero (that's a cliché). Always hands + ingredient + action.
**Don't reuse**: The Mexican terroir specifics.
**Why**: Family-business humanization without the "about us" stock-photo trap.

### 5.2 Geranium ingredient moments — https://www.geranium.dk/
**Visual**: Extreme close-ups of herbs, oils beading, salt crystals. Almost microscopic. These break up the plate-hero rhythm.
**Reuse**: **Macro interstitials** — every 2-3 plate shots, a near-abstract macro of a single texture. Breaks visual monotony, adds luxury pacing.
**Don't reuse**: Cold Nordic color grading.
**Why**: Teaches the photography rhythm: PLATE → MACRO → ENVIRONMENT → PLATE. Never 4 plates in a row.

### 5.3 Oteque dish portraits — https://www.oteque.com/
**Visual**: Each dish shot at a consistent 3/4 angle against a neutral backdrop. Editorial rigor. Feels like a catalog, in a good way.
**Reuse**: **Consistency rule** — every dish photo uses the same angle, lens, lighting, and surface. The buffet's visual signature comes from this rigor, not from prop variety.
**Don't reuse**: The coldness.
**Why**: Lock this as a photography brief rule for the Di Matoso production day.

## 6. Editorial Motion — Plate Reveals, Parallax Text, Scroll Storytelling

### 6.1 Noma scroll behavior — https://noma.dk/
**Visual**: Lenis-style smooth scroll. Images fade up slowly (1.2s+). Text and image arrive on different beats. No bouncing, no springy physics.
**Reuse**: Slow fade-ups (1000-1400ms), text and image staggered by ~300ms, Lenis smooth scroll. Zero spring physics, zero bounce.
**Don't reuse**: N/A.
**Why**: Sets the motion tempo for Di Matoso: SLOW. Luxury moves slowly. Speed is for SaaS.

### 6.2 Geranium plate reveals — https://www.geranium.dk/
**Visual**: Plates appear via subtle clip-path reveals from bottom, synced with the plate's cinematography. Text overlays fade in after the plate is resolved.
**Reuse**: **Clip-path bottom-up reveal** for every plate-hero, paired with text-after-image staggering (image resolves, then italic dish name fades in, then ingredient list).
**Don't reuse**: Their specific 3D tilt (too much).
**Why**: Signature motion moment. Every plate arrives like a waiter setting it down.

### 6.3 Mirazur parallax text over photo — https://www.mirazur.fr/
**Visual**: Large serif quotes parallax over full-bleed photography at ~0.6x scroll speed. Creates depth without gimmick.
**Reuse**: **0.6x parallax** for serif quote pulls over full-bleed food photography. Only for quotes, never for body copy.
**Don't reuse**: Their specific photograph overlays.
**Why**: One controlled parallax technique — used sparingly, earns its place.

### 6.4 Restaurant GEM motion — https://www.awwwards.com/sites/restaurant-gem
**Visual**: Subtle section transitions, cursor magnetism on CTAs, hairline rules that draw themselves on scroll.
**Reuse**: **Self-drawing hairline rules** — the gold rules (section dividers, underlines) draw themselves from left-to-right on scroll entry. Tiny but expensive-feeling.
**Don't reuse**: The full cursor magnetism (feels agency-y when overused).
**Why**: One signature scroll-in micro-interaction for the gold rules.

### 6.5 Eleven Madison Park editorial pacing — https://www.elevenmadisonpark.com/
**Visual**: Each section is a "page turn" — not literal, but the composition fully resolves before you scroll past. No half-states.
**Reuse**: **Section as page** — each scroll section occupies a full viewport and composes fully before the next. No mid-scroll half-reveal states.
**Don't reuse**: N/A.
**Why**: Structural rule: the site reads like a book, not a feed.

## Direção recomendada pro Di Matoso

Pro art-director puxar na Phase 2 do Protocolo Branding. Opiniões, não sugestões:

1. **Palette lock: #1A1A1A charcoal + #B8860B-ish deep gold (editorial, never saturated) + #FAFAF7 cream + burgundy whisper (#3D1F1F) as tertiary.** Gold vive exclusivamente em hairlines, underlines e numerais — NUNCA como fill de botão, NUNCA como background de bloco. Se o gold virar fill, a marca virou tacky. Esta é a regra inegociável.

2. **Typography lock: Cormorant Garamond (display + italic) + Inter (structural).** Italic é voz, não ornamento — reservado para descrições poéticas de pratos, quotes de família, subtítulos editoriais. Inter carrega ingredientes, preços, datas, navegação. Binário rígido. Nada de terceira face.

3. **Photography rhythm lock: PLATE → MACRO → ENVIRONMENT → HANDS → PLATE.** Nunca 4 pratos seguidos. Fotografia de mãos (preparo, finalização, corte) é humanização obrigatória — sem closeup do rosto do chef da família como hero (cliché). Ângulo 3/4 consistente para pratos, luz quente natural, fundo em pedra/madeira/tecido linho neutros.

4. **Menu as editorial manuscript**, não delivery list. Cada "menu de evento" é um ensaio: nome temático ("Colheita," "Festança," "Raízes"), 2 parágrafos de narrativa, cursos como cards numerados com algarismo romano, descrição italic, ingredientes sans, **zero preço visível** — CTA único "solicitar orçamento" como linha serif sublinhada, não como pill button.

5. **Motion lock: Lenis smooth scroll, 1000-1400ms fade-ups, clip-path bottom-up reveals para cada prato-hero, 0.6x parallax para quotes serif sobre full-bleed photography, self-drawing hairlines em gold nas entradas de seção.** Zero spring, zero bounce, zero física saltitante. Luxo se move devagar.

6. **Structural motif: Roman numerals como âncoras editoriais.** Cada seção, cada curso, cada capítulo da página tem seu algarismo romano hairline em gold na lateral. Rouba direto de Atomix + EMP. Vira assinatura visual do Di Matoso.

7. **Hero pattern: uma única foto gigante de prato OU um par de mãos em ação, zero UI chrome sobre a imagem, título serif no canto inferior esquerdo em 2-3 linhas, CTA serif underline no canto oposto.** A foto é o headline. Se a foto não aguenta ser headline, a foto não é boa o suficiente — brief manda refazer o shoot, não adiciona copy pra compensar.

8. **Section-as-page rule (EMP):** cada seção do site ocupa viewport inteiro e compõe completamente antes da próxima. Nada de "half-reveal" no meio do scroll. O site é lido como um livro de chef, não como um feed. Isso força disciplina brutal em quantidade de seções (máximo 6-7 no longform) e em hierarquia de uma ideia por tela.

## Top 3 Aesthetic Directions (recap for delivery)

1. **Editorial manuscript** — menu-as-essay + Roman numerals + serif italic voice + section-as-page. Rouba de Atomix + EMP + Noma.
2. **Deep gold discipline** — gold exclusivamente em hairlines/underlines/numerais, charcoal+cream carregam o peso, burgundy whisper de acento terciário. Rouba de Le Meurice Ducasse.
3. **Slow cinematic photography rhythm** — PLATE→MACRO→ENVIRONMENT→HANDS loop, clip-path reveals, 1000-1400ms fades, Lenis. Rouba de Geranium + Mirazur + Noma.
