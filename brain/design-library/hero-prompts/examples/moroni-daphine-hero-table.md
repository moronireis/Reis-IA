# Moroni & Daphine — Hero Variant C: Mesa posta com velas altas e runner bordô

Section: o-grande-dia / galeria hero
Intent: Wide-medium da mesa posta antes dos convidados chegarem. Candelabros altos, runner de veludo, taças de cristal, cartões de lugar em caligrafia. A imagem que vende "grande dia formal europeu".
Tools tested on: Gemini 2.5 Flash Image / Flux 1.1 Pro / Midjourney v7

## Prompt

A medium-wide fine-art still life of a formal wedding reception table photographed from a low three-quarter angle, just above table height, looking down the length of the table into the warm distance. A deep burgundy velvet runner (#4A1619) runs the full length of a long cream linen tablecloth (#F4ECDC). Three antique brass candelabra stand along the runner at regular intervals, each holding five ivory taper candles in full flame, the wax slightly dripped. Crystal coupes and burgundy wine glasses catch the candlelight in prismatic glints. Place settings include hand-calligraphed name cards on heavy cream card stock, antique gold flatware, and small clusters of dark bordeaux garden roses with eucalyptus sprigs at each place. The far end of the table fades into warm bokeh where more candles glow as indistinct golden blooms.

Lighting is entirely practical — the actual candles on the table are the only light source, supplemented only by a very subtle warm bounce from cream walls implied out of frame. Color temperature 2500-2700K. Deep chiaroscuro: the foreground place setting is in warm glowing focus, the mid-ground candles are the brightest points, and everything beyond the third candelabra falls into dreamy dark burgundy shadow. Warm haze fills the air between candelabras, making each flame's halo visible. Dust motes and the faintest wisp of candle smoke catch the light.

Shot on a Contax 645 with 80mm f/2 lens, aperture wide open, film stock Kodak Portra 400 pushed +1 stop for maximum warmth and grain. Shallow depth of field — the frontmost place setting is crisp, the second candelabra is already softly blurred, the third is painterly bokeh. Visible 35mm color negative grain throughout. Color balance tungsten warm, +20 warm, -12 cyan, with 10% gold radial overlay from center (heavier than rings/florals variants because the scene is larger). Strong halation glow around every candle flame.

Mood: Chateau Pichon Baron dining room, Rocco Forte Valentino bar at Hotel de la Ville Rome, the hour before guests arrive when the candles have just been lit and the room is holding its breath. Style anchors: Junebug Weddings reception feature, Four Seasons Private Events, Ralph Lauren Romance dinner scene, Dior Fall campaign interiors.

## Negative

- no pastel pink, no millennial rose, no lavender, no lilac, no baby's breath
- no bright yellow gold, no chrome, no shiny new metal, no #FFD700
- no cool lighting, no overhead fluorescent, no daylight through window, no blue hour
- no pure black (#000000) — always warm charcoal or burgundy shadow
- no electric blue, no teal, no cyan, no REIS [IA] palette
- no chess imagery, no crown, no H1-B hourglass, no Z7
- no people in frame, no hands, no waitstaff, no chefs
- no plates of food served, no half-eaten scene — table is PRISTINE and untouched
- no Canva wedding template, no Minted, no Zola default
- no paper napkins, no plastic, no cheap candles, no tealights only
- no modern minimal tablescape, no Scandinavian hygge, no rustic farmhouse
- no sparklers, no string lights, no fairy lights, no bulbs in jars
- no text, no watermarks, no borders
- no HDR, no oversharp digital, no overexposed flame, no crushed shadows

## Post-processing

- Warm overlay: `radial-gradient(circle at 50% 55%, rgba(212,175,55,0.10), transparent 75%)`
- Film grain: 35mm color negative, opacity 0.14, blend mode overlay
- Vignette: warm charcoal (#1A1312) at 22% toward corners — heavier vignette appropriate for wider shot
- Halation on every candle flame (Gaussian blur 4px, 30% opacity, screen blend)
- Optional: very subtle volumetric haze overlay to reinforce practical candlelight atmosphere
