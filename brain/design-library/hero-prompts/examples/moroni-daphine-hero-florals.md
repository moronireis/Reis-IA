# Moroni & Daphine — Hero Variant B: Buquê de rosas borgonha e calla em penumbra

Section: hero / nossa-historia transition
Intent: A densidade floral que Daphine pediu, traduzida em luz de vela. Rosas bordeaux pesadas, calla creme e eucalipto, sobre linho creme marcado. Referência direta a Dutch still-life.
Tools tested on: Gemini 2.5 Flash Image / Flux 1.1 Pro / Midjourney v7

## Prompt

A medium-close fine-art still life of a dense romantic bouquet — deep burgundy garden roses at full bloom, their petals curling open and heavy, mixed with cream calla lilies whose throats glow with warm reflected light, soft sprigs of preserved eucalyptus and one single dried bordeaux rose petal fallen on the surface below. The arrangement is tied loosely with a matte cream silk ribbon, the knot falling to the left of frame. Placed on aged cream linen (#F4ECDC) with visible weave and wrinkles, on a dark wood table whose grain disappears into shadow.

Lighting is single warm candle source at 2700K coming from the upper-right at a steep 55-degree angle, creating gentle chiaroscuro — the front-facing petals are illuminated with golden warmth while the back of the bouquet falls into deep burgundy shadow. A soft warm cream bounce on the left keeps the shadows readable but never cold. Practical candles visible as out-of-focus golden bokeh in the far background, blurred into blooms of warm light. Warm haze visible in the beam of key light, a few dust motes catching the edge of the brightest petals.

Shot on a Pentax 67 with 105mm f/2.4 lens, aperture f/2, film stock Fuji 400H pushed +1 for warmth. Shallow depth of field — the frontmost rose is crisp, the back of the bouquet already dissolving into painterly blur. Prominent 35mm color negative grain. Color balance tungsten warm, +15 warm -10 cyan, subtle 8% gold radial overlay (Ralph Lauren Romance treatment). Slight halation glow on the cream calla lily's throat where it catches the key light.

Mood: opulent, silent, Dutch-painting reverence — flowers as protagonists, not decoration, the way Rachel Ruysch and Willem van Aelst painted them, the way Magnolia Rouge photographs them. The whole frame feels like the last hour of afternoon in a wine cellar. Style anchors: Magnolia Rouge bordeaux feature, Dutch still-life masters, Junebug Weddings romantic luxury, Once Wed winter editorial gallery.

## Negative

- no pastel pink roses, no millennial rose, no lavender, no lilac, no baby's breath
- no bright yellow gold, no chrome, no metallic gradient, no #FFD700
- no cool lighting, no blue hour, no daylight white balance, no fluorescent overhead
- no pure black (#000000) background — always warm charcoal or burgundy
- no electric blue, no teal, no cyan, no REIS [IA] palette
- no chess imagery, no crown, no H1-B hourglass, no Z7
- no happy stock couple, no hands, no people in frame
- no hearts, no confetti, no watercolor style, no illustration, no clip-art
- no Canva wedding template, no Minted default, no Zola default
- no HDR, no oversharp digital, no overexposed highlights, no crushed shadows
- no sparse minimalist ikebana — this is abundance, not restraint
- no text, no watermarks, no borders, no vignette frame

## Post-processing

- Warm overlay: `radial-gradient(circle at 50% 40%, rgba(212,175,55,0.08), transparent 75%)`
- Film grain: 35mm color negative, opacity 0.14, blend mode overlay
- Vignette: warm charcoal (#1A1312) at 20% toward corners
- Slight halation on warmest highlight (Gaussian 3px at 20% opacity, screen blend)
