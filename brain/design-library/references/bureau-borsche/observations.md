## What makes this premium

Bureau Borsche is the standard-bearer for magazine-spread web composition. The studio treats every page as an editorial layout — massive display type set at unexpected anchors, deliberately asymmetric composition, strong negative space, and the confidence to let a single word carry an entire frame. Where every other reference in the Top 10 works within a grid, Bureau Borsche breaks the grid intentionally and makes the breakage the composition. This is the most aggressive Swiss-editorial reference in the set and it represents the ceiling of what typographic confidence can look like on the web. For REIS [IA], it's a north star for the hero headline treatment specifically — the kind of discipline where "Crescimento com IA" can occupy an entire viewport at the right type scale.

## What to steal (for REIS [IA])

1. **Massive display type (150px+) at the hero** — beyond Pentagram's already-large scale. Hero headline becomes the composition itself.
2. **Asymmetric typographic anchor points** — hero headline should not be centered. Anchor it to the left rule with generous right margin, or to a disciplined offset from a 12-col grid position. This is the move that reads as editorial rather than marketing.
3. **Magazine-spread section metaphor** — each major section of the REIS home feels like a spread, with a clear type anchor, generous margin, and a single visual subject. Even without scroll-snap, the composition can honor this metaphor.
4. **Negative space as compositional element** — the empty half of a frame is doing work. It's not "unused space" — it's the part of the composition that gives the occupied part its weight.

## What to leave behind

- Any actual scroll-snap paging (too gimmicky for consulting-firm tone)
- The occasional strong accent color (red/yellow in Borsche work) — violates REIS brand lock
- Typographic experimentation that reads as "design studio showing off" — REIS is a consulting firm, not an agency
