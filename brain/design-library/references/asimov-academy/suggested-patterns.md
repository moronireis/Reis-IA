# Suggested Patterns — asimov.academy → brain/design-library/patterns/

**Date**: 2026-04-14

## Verdict

Asimov Academy yields **zero visual/motion patterns** worth distilling into the REIS [IA] design library. Its visual/motion craft is below the floor we are targeting.

However, it yields **three structural/conversion patterns** worth capturing as *information architecture patterns* (not visual patterns):

---

## Pattern 1 — Stat Bar as Social Proof Sub-Hero

**Name**: `ia-social-proof-stat-bar`
**Type**: Information architecture / hero composition
**Origin**: Asimov uses `+18 mil alunos · 275h aulas · 61 cursos · 79 projetos` immediately after the H1 as a first-fold trust layer.

**Adapted for REIS [IA]**: Replace student-centric metrics with revenue/outcome metrics.
Example: `R$ 12M em receita atribuída · 40+ implementações · 3 anos de framework · 0 prototypes no graveyard`

**Why worth capturing**: High-conversion edtech convention. Executive buyers pattern-match to stat bars faster than to paragraph copy.

---

## Pattern 2 — Three-Tier Curriculum Map

**Name**: `ia-three-tier-pillar-map`
**Type**: Information architecture
**Origin**: Asimov's Formação → Trilha → Curso hierarchy lets visitors self-select depth of commitment.

**Adapted for REIS [IA]**: Pillar → Program → Outcome map.
Builders (mentorship) | Systems (done-for-you) | Marketing (demand engine), each with a progression rail.

**Why worth capturing**: Gives visitors a mental model of "where do I enter" in 5 seconds. Asimov's implementation is visually weak, but the information pattern is strong.

---

## Pattern 3 — Single Funnel, Many Doors

**Name**: `ia-funnel-convergence`
**Type**: Conversion pattern
**Origin**: Every Asimov CTA routes to one Hotmart checkout regardless of which formacao the visitor entered from.

**Adapted for REIS [IA]**: Every CTA routes to `/agendar` or `/aplicar` — already locked in brand-audit-checklist.md. Asimov confirms the pattern works in the wild.

**Why worth capturing**: Validation that our existing funnel rule (no pricing tables, one conversation door) is not a constraint but a best practice shared by high-conversion edtech.
