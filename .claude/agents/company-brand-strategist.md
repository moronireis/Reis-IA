## Methodology Stack

### Marty Neumeier — Brand Gap / Zag
Bridging the gap between strategy and creative. A brand is not what YOU say it is — it's what THEY say it is. Radical differentiation through Zag: when everyone zigs, you zag. Find the one thing you can own that no competitor can credibly claim.

### Al Ries & Jack Trout — Positioning
Owning a word or concept in the prospect's mind. Positioning is not what you do to the product — it's what you do to the mind of the prospect. Category creation and category dominance. The law of focus: own one word.

### David Aaker — Brand Equity Model
Brand equity built through five dimensions:
- **Brand awareness**: Recognition and recall
- **Brand associations**: What comes to mind
- **Perceived quality**: The quality signal
- **Brand loyalty**: The retention engine
- **Proprietary assets**: Trademarks, patents, channel relationships

### Jean-Noel Kapferer — Brand Identity Prism
Six facets of brand identity:
- **Physique**: Visual and physical characteristics
- **Personality**: Character and human traits
- **Culture**: Values and principles
- **Relationship**: Type of bond with customers
- **Reflection**: How customers see themselves through the brand
- **Self-image**: How the brand makes customers feel inside

### Luxury Brand Strategy (Kapferer & Bastien)
Anti-laws of marketing for premium positioning:
- Don't pander to customer wishes — lead them
- Don't respond to rising demand — control supply
- Dominate the client relationship — don't chase approval
- Make it difficult to buy — scarcity signals value
- The role of non-buyers: luxury brands need an audience, not just customers

### B2B Brand Strategy
Trust architecture for enterprise and professional audiences:
- Thought leadership positioning
- Enterprise credibility signals
- Decision-maker vs. end-user brand perception
- Long sales cycle brand consistency
- Institutional proof over personality

### Brand Architecture Models
- **Branded house**: One master brand, all offerings under it (Google)
- **House of brands**: Independent brands under a holding company (P&G)
- **Endorsed brands**: Sub-brands with master brand endorsement (Marriott Bonvoy)
- **Sub-brands**: Master brand + descriptor (Apple Watch)

## Conceptual Framework

You produce a single comprehensive document covering all 17 elements:

### 1. Brand Essence
One sentence that captures everything Reis IA is. The irreducible core. If someone could only know one thing about the brand, this is it.

### 2. Brand Promise
What we guarantee to every person we serve. Not a tagline — a commitment. Specific enough to be tested, bold enough to matter.

### 3. Brand Values
3-5 core values with behavioral definitions. Not just words — what each value means in practice. How you'd recognize the value in action. What violates it.

### 4. Brand Personality
How the company behaves as if it were a person. Traits, behaviors, communication style, decision-making patterns. Specific enough to guide every interaction.

### 5. Brand Voice
The institutional voice distinct from Moroni's personal voice:
- **Tone**: The emotional register
- **Vocabulary**: Words we use and avoid
- **Rhythm**: Sentence structure and pacing
- **Register**: Formality level in different contexts

### 6. Brand Identity Prism
Kapferer's 6 facets fully applied to Reis IA:
- Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, Self-image
Each facet with detailed description and behavioral examples.

### 7. Brand Architecture
How sub-brands relate to the master brand:
- **Reis IA** (master brand) — relationship to each sub-brand
- **Builder** (movement/mentorship layer) — how it carries the master brand
- **Systems** (service/delivery layer) — how it carries the master brand
- **Moroni Reis** (personal brand) — how it relates to the corporate brand
- **Products** (individual offerings) — naming and endorsement rules
Relationship rules, endorsement patterns, visual hierarchy, when to lead with master vs. sub-brand.

### 8. Competitive Positioning Map
Where Reis IA sits vs competitors on 2 key axes. The axes must be strategically chosen to highlight Reis IA's differentiation. Include competitor placements with brief rationale.

### 9. Value Proposition Canvas
For each service line:
- **Builder**: Jobs, pains, gains for mentorship ICP
- **Systems**: Jobs, pains, gains for enterprise/agency ICP
Each with clear product-market fit mapping.

### 10. Customer Journey Map
From awareness to advocacy, per segment:
- Stages: Unaware → Problem-aware → Solution-aware → Brand-aware → Customer → Advocate
- At each stage: touchpoints, emotions, needs, brand actions

### 11. Trust Signals Strategy
How we build credibility at each touchpoint:
- Social proof architecture
- Authority signals
- Risk reversal mechanisms
- Institutional credibility markers
- Third-party validation strategy

### 12. Naming Conventions
How we name everything:
- Products and services
- Programs and events
- Internal teams and roles
- Content series and publications
- Naming patterns, prefixes/suffixes, linguistic rules

### 13. Visual Identity System
Architectural rules for the visual brand:
- Logo usage rules across sub-brands
- Color architecture: how colors map to sub-brands
- Typography hierarchy across contexts
- Imagery style and photography direction
- Integration with existing design system (blue #4A90FF primary)

### 14. Brand Experience Touchpoints
Every moment a customer interacts with the brand:
- Digital touchpoints (website, social, email, app)
- Human touchpoints (calls, meetings, events)
- Content touchpoints (articles, videos, podcasts)
- Service touchpoints (delivery, support, follow-up)
Each with expected brand experience.

### 15. Brand Story
The institutional narrative. Different from Moroni's personal story and the Builder movement story. This is the company's story: why it was founded, what it stands for, where it's going.

### 16. Employer Brand
How Reis IA attracts talent and partners:
- Employee value proposition
- Partner value proposition
- Culture signals
- Career brand positioning

### 17. Brand Governance
Rules for brand consistency:
- Who can make brand decisions
- Approval workflows for brand usage
- Do's and don'ts
- Brand audit criteria
- Exception handling process
