## Methodology Stack

### April Dunford — Obviously Awesome
Positioning as competitive context, not features. The best positioning makes the value obvious by framing the product against the right competitive alternatives. Context determines value perception. Don't describe what you are — describe what you replace and why you're better.

### Strategyzer — Value Proposition Design
Jobs-to-be-done, pains, and gains mapping. Every product must address a specific job the customer is trying to get done, relieve specific pains, and create specific gains. The product-market fit is visible when the value map matches the customer profile.

### Product-Led Growth Branding
The product experience IS the brand (adapted for service model). Every interaction with the product — from first touch to completion — shapes brand perception. The delivery IS the marketing.

### Luxury Product Marketing
Scarcity, craftsmanship narrative, and experience over features:
- The product story is about WHO made it and HOW, not just what it does
- Scarcity signals value — limited access, selective admission
- The experience of buying and using is as important as the outcome
- Packaging matters — how the product is presented signals its worth

### Naming Methodology
Five naming approaches with evaluation criteria:
- **Descriptive**: Says what it is (straightforward, less ownable)
- **Suggestive**: Hints at what it does (evocative, more ownable)
- **Abstract**: Unrelated to function (fully ownable, requires more marketing)
- **Coined**: Made-up word (completely ownable, requires brand building)
- **Acronym**: Letters forming a word (memorable if done well, forgettable if not)

Evaluation criteria for every name: phonetic appeal, cultural fit (Portuguese context), memorability, digital availability, trademark potential, emotional resonance.

### Brand Extension Theory
When and how to extend a brand vs. create something new:
- Extension: when the master brand adds credibility
- New brand: when the master brand adds baggage or limits positioning
- Sub-brand: when you need both the parent's trust and the child's distinctiveness
