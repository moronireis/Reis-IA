# Brand Architecture Decisions Log

## 2026-03-18 — v1.0 Framework Creation

### Architecture Model Choice: Endorsed Sub-Brands
- Chose endorsed model over branded house (too restrictive for distinct audiences) and house of brands (too disconnected for early stage)
- Endorsed model allows each sub-brand distinct personality while master brand provides credibility
- Real-world parallel: Marriott (master endorses distinct hotel brands), Virgin (master endorses diverse ventures)

### Color Architecture Rationale (updated 2026-03-20 — gold/amber/terracotta deprecated)
- **Time Builders Electric Blue (#2D7AFF) + Cyan (#00B4FF)**: High energy, movement. Electric Blue for primary accent, Cyan for peak energy moments.
- **Systems (#4A90FF minimal)**: Black/white dominant. Blue used with extreme restraint — max 1 element per viewport. Premium communicated by absence of color.
- **Moroni Soft Blue (#6AADFF)**: Warmer, softer blue. Human, accessible. Bridges personal brand to ecosystem.
- **Blue thread principle**: Every layer includes blue at different intensities to maintain ecosystem coherence.
- **DEPRECATED (2026-03-18)**: Builder amber (#FF8C42), Systems gold (#C4A265), Moroni terracotta (#C47A5A). All warm tones removed from brand in v2.0/v3.0 revision.

### Naming Decisions
- Sub-brands always prefixed in formal contexts: "Reis IA Builder," "Reis IA Systems"
- No SaaS tier language ever (Pro/Plus/Enterprise)
- Product names: action-oriented for Builder, outcome-oriented for Systems
- English for sub-brand names, Portuguese for product names in Brazilian market

### Key Governance Rules
- Sub-brands do not endorse each other (cross-refs go through master brand)
- Visual identity does not cross (Builder page never looks like Systems page)
- Moroni bridges (creates awareness), company anchors (converts and retains)
- All results aggregate upward to master brand proof points
- Every page leads to /agendar or /aplicar — no dead ends
