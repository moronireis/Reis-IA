# Naming Decisions Log

## Builder Mentorship Program

### Chosen: FORJA (Forge)
- Full name: Reis IA Builder Forja
- Rationale: Metaphor alignment with Builder identity, visceral transformation narrative, "Forjado" as graduate title works as badge of honor, two syllables, works perfectly in PT-BR
- Graduate title: Forjado (plural: Forjados)
- Cohort naming: Turma + Roman numeral (Turma I, Turma II, etc.)

### Rejected alternatives and why:
- **Arsenal**: Too combative, less sophisticated for premium tier
- **Comando**: Military metaphor may feel performative without truly selective admission
- **Travessia**: Beautiful but too contemplative; lacks the punch Builder brand needs
- **Origem**: Elegant but too passive; the program is about building, not just beginning

### Phase names (metallurgical stages):
1. Materia-Prima (Raw Material) — Weeks 1-2
2. Fundicao (Smelting) — Weeks 3-4
3. Forjamento (The Forging) — Weeks 5-6
4. Tempera (Tempering) — Weeks 7-10
5. Acabamento (Finishing) — Weeks 11-12

## Systems Products

### Naming convention: Reis IA Systems [Function] [Suffix]
- Three locked suffixes: Engine, Agent, Protocol
- Function names always in English (Revenue, Support, Qualification, etc.)
- Names never translated to Portuguese

### Product categories (not quality tiers):
- Protocol = diagnostics/audits (R$2.5K-R$7.5K)
- Agent = single-function AI (R$10K-R$25K)
- Engine = integrated multi-agent systems (R$25K-R$100K)

### Delivery stages:
Diagnostico > Arquitetura > Construcao > Calibracao > Lancamento > Vigilia

### Support levels:
1. Vigilia Estendida (Extended Watch)
2. Otimizacao Ativa (Active Optimization)
3. Comando de Operacoes (Operations Command)

## Cultural/Linguistic Notes
- Portuguese words with strong consonant openings (F in Forja, C in Comando) carry more weight
- Metallurgical vocabulary is universally understood in Brazilian Portuguese
- "Turma" carries warmth that "cohort" or "batch" lacks — preferred for group naming
- English suffixes (Engine, Agent, Protocol) are already normalized in Brazilian B2B vocabulary
