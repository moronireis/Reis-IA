Patterns validated through framework knowledge and Leilaine's brand voice application (2026-04-24):

**Pattern 1 — Diagnosis opener (works best for Leilaine's Sage-Ruler archetype)**
"[Company does X]. But [diagnostic question that reveals hidden problem]."
Example: "Sua empresa fatura. Às vezes bem. Mas se eu te pedir para prever o próximo trimestre com margem de 15%, você consegue?"
Why: Makes the reader self-diagnose before reading. Creates personal relevance instantly.

**Pattern 2 — Contrarian declaration**
"[Expert consensus]. Vou dizer algo que [authority category] não diz."
Example: "Vou dizer algo que a maioria dos consultores de IA não diz. A maioria das empresas de construção civil não está pronta para IA."
Why: Polarizes — people who agree feel seen, people who disagree comment. Both drive dwell time.

**Pattern 3 — Specific incident opener**
"[Client anonymized + specific number]. [The diagnosis that surprised them.]"
Example: "Uma metalúrgica no interior de SP faturava R$180K por mês... E não conseguia prever se o próximo mês seria R$160K ou R$220K."
Why: Specificity signals real experience. Readers recognize their own situation in the client's.

**Pattern 4 — Label the feeling / name the invisible**
"[Behavior everyone recognizes]. Isso não é [what people call it]. É [real name]."
Example: "'Falta de tempo' é a mentira mais cara da construção civil."
Why: Naming what people feel but couldn't articulate creates instant resonance. They share because it says what they couldn't.

**Anti-patterns to avoid for Leilaine specifically:**
- Never start with "Você sabia que..." (too much like a quiz)
- Never start with a question that can be answered "no" and closed ("Você usa CRM?")
- Never use "Hoje quero falar sobre..." (AI pattern, kills dwell time)

**How to apply:** For Leilaine's voice, always prefer Pattern 1 or 4 on Monday posts (authority-building). Pattern 2 or 3 on Wednesday (education + distribution). Pattern 4 on Friday (emotional resonance).
