description: Reis IA is a high-ticket consultancy, never display SaaS pricing tables, plan comparisons, or self-serve checkout patterns

NEVER display pricing tables, plan comparisons, subscription-style layouts, tier cards (Basic/Pro/Enterprise), monthly/annual toggles, or per-seat pricing anywhere on the Reis IA site.

Every CTA must route to either `/agendar` (booking) or `/aplicar` (application). The conversion model is always: visitor → conversation with team, never visitor → self-checkout.

The visual language should feel like a premium consulting firm or luxury brand, not a software product page.

When extracting or applying design patterns from SaaS references (Stripe, Linear, Vercel, etc.), use only their design craft — ignore SaaS-specific patterns (pricing grids, feature comparison tables, free trial CTAs, login/signup flows).

**Why:** Reis IA sells high-ticket consulting and implementation services. SaaS patterns undermine the premium positioning and confuse the conversion model.

**How to apply:** This applies permanently to every stage and phase. When building pages, designing components, or extracting references — always filter out SaaS commercial patterns. Every conversion path ends at `/agendar` or `/aplicar`.
